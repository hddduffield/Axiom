# Document Architecture — Plan Output Structure

The standard PSA Wealth plan deliverable follows this architecture, derived from the Holloway exemplar.

## STANDARD SECTIONS (in order)

### 1. Cover Page
- Client name(s)
- "Financial Plan" or "Plan Update"
- Plan version / date
- "Prepared by PSA Wealth"
- Compliance ID

### 2. Executive Summary (1-2 pages)
- Plan purpose / engagement
- Key recommendations summary (5-10 bullets max)
- Plan navigation
- Reading guidance

### 3. Your Situation (3-5 pages)
- Family status
- Business and entity status
- Estate documents in place
- Insurance in place
- Retirement and benefits in place
- Investment status
- Charitable activity
- Current top-of-mind items / questions

### 4. Goals and Priorities (1-2 pages)
- Stated goals from fact review
- Priority ordering
- Time horizon for each

### 5. Recommendations (15-40 pages, depending on archetype)

Subsections by category:
- Personal Tax
- Business Tax
- Entity Structure
- Estate Planning
- Risk & Insurance
- Retirement & Benefits
- Investment & Cash
- Succession & Continuity
- Charitable Planning
- Family
- Specialty (if applicable)

Each recommendation block:
- Recommendation statement (from Plan Output Template)
- Quantified impact
- Mechanic
- Coordination roles
- Timing

### 6. Pre-Transaction Sequence (Pre-Exit only) (3-6 pages)
- Timeline graphic or list
- Stage-by-stage breakdown
- Critical dates and dependencies

### 7. Implementation Timeline (1-2 pages)
- Quarter-by-quarter or milestone view
- Who's responsible for each
- Dependencies

### 8. Strategies Considered But Not Included (1-2 pages)
- Strategies discussed and rejected
- Reason for rejection
- Conditions under which we'd revisit

This is important — it documents that we considered (and rejected) landmines and other inappropriate strategies.

### 9. Open Items & Decisions Needed (1 page)
- Specific decisions client needs to make
- Information still needed
- Next meeting agenda

### 10. Disclosures and Disclaimers (2-3 pages)
- MML disclosure (verbatim)
- Tax / legal disclaimer
- Estimates / projections disclaimer
- Section-specific disclosures
- Compliance ID

## TYPOGRAPHY & FORMATTING

Per the Holloway plan (and Will's preference):
- Single-column layout
- Section headers prominent
- Sub-recommendation headers second-level
- Body text 11-12pt
- Recommendation blocks visually distinct (could be light box, indent, or simply spacing)
- Numerical values bold or otherwise emphasized
- Citations in footnotes or end-references (not inline parentheticals that disrupt reading)

## TABLES

Use tables for:
- Multi-step sequencing (year, action, role)
- Cost-benefit comparisons
- Coverage levels (insurance) with current vs. recommended
- Tax bracket scenarios
- Asset location (account → holdings)

Avoid tables for narrative content. The plan reads as document, not spreadsheet.

## VISUAL HIERARCHY

Guideline: someone scanning the plan should be able to:
- Identify recommendations in 30 seconds (from headers / TOC)
- Get the "shape" of the work in 5 minutes (from executive summary + section headers)
- Find a specific topic in 2 minutes (from TOC)

If plan readers can't do this, formatting needs improvement.

## PLAN LENGTH BY ARCHETYPE

- PRE-EXIT: 40-60 pages
- POST-EXIT: 35-50 pages
- ACTIVE-NO-EXIT: 30-45 pages
- FAMILY-OFFICE: 50-80 pages
- PRE-LIQUIDITY-FOUNDER: 25-40 pages

These are guidelines. A focused 30-page plan beats an 80-page kitchen-sink document.
