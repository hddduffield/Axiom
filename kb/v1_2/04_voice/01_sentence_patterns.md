# Voice — Sentence Patterns

## CORE STYLE

The PSA Wealth voice is:
- **Direct** — answers stated; no hedging that obscures the recommendation
- **Plain** — technical concepts translated; jargon minimized; explanatory when used
- **Quantified** — specific dollar amounts, specific years, specific rates
- **Owner-respectful** — assumes intelligence; explains rationale; doesn't condescend
- **Coordinated** — references CPA, attorney, banker as the team; doesn't claim authority outside our lane

## SENTENCE PATTERN A — Direct Recommendation

Structure: "We recommend [specific action]. [Quantified impact]. [Mechanic in 1-2 sentences]. [What it requires of client]."

Example:
> "We recommend electing Georgia's pass-through entity tax for {entity_name}. At your level of pass-through income (~$1.2M), the annual federal benefit is approximately $44,000. The election is made annually on the entity return; quarterly estimated payments must come from the entity's tax account, not personal funds. Your CPA files; we coordinate quarterly."

## SENTENCE PATTERN B — Trade-off Acknowledgment

Structure: "[Strategy] [does X]. The trade-off is [Y]. We're comfortable with that here because [specific reason for this client]."

Example:
> "Cross-purchase gives the surviving owner a basis step-up at the buyout — meaningful at any future sale. The trade-off is administrative complexity at 4+ owners. We're comfortable with that here because there are only two of you."

## SENTENCE PATTERN C — Sequencing Setup

Structure: "Before [later step], we need [earlier step]. [Reason]."

Example:
> "Before the F-reorganization, we need to separate the real estate (REC-ENT-001). The Holdco that emerges from the reorganization should hold operating only — clean separation simplifies every downstream structure."

## SENTENCE PATTERN D — Quantified Magnitude

Structure: "[Strategy] [saves/transfers] approximately [$X]. [How calculated]."

Example:
> "The IDGT sale transfers approximately $10M-$15M of growth to the children's trusts over the 9-year note. That's the difference between the asset's appreciation rate (~12%) and the AFR note rate (~4.5%) over the term, plus any subsequent transaction event."

## SENTENCE PATTERN E — Quarterback Coordination

Structure: "PSA [our role]. [CPA / attorney / specialist] [their role]. [Who handles what specifically]."

Example:
> "PSA models the projection and coordinates the funding. Your CPA handles the §409A analysis and the annual W-2 imputed income calculation. Your attorney drafts the plan document. We meet quarterly to keep the pieces aligned."

## SENTENCE PATTERN F — Plan Output Section Opener

Structure: "[Section descriptor]. [Brief reason this section matters for this client]."

Example:
> "**Estate Planning.** With expected exemption sunset behind us (OBBBA made $15M permanent) and your projected estate at $50M+, the goal isn't avoiding estate tax entirely — it's ensuring the family receives the maximum economic benefit while you and Catherine retain control of the plan during your lifetimes."

## WORD-LEVEL CHOICES

### Prefer
- "We recommend" (not "you should consider")
- "The annual benefit is approximately $X" (not "this could potentially save")
- "Your CPA" (not "a tax professional")
- "Pre-LOI" (not "before a definitive agreement")
- "Qualified appraisal" (not "valuation")
- "Sale to grantor trust" (not "intra-family sale")

### Avoid
- "Consider whether you might want to potentially..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Going forward..."
- "It is worth mentioning..."
- "Please be advised..."
- Excessive jargon without translation

### Numbers and dates
- Use specific years where applicable: "by April 15, 2027" not "next April"
- Use $ABS not "K" or "M" in plan body for HNW clarity (write "$1,200,000" or "$1.2M" — both acceptable; pick one and stick)
- Decimal places: 2 for percentages with rate-sensitive math; 1 for general
- Round magnitudes: "$15M-$20M of additional wealth transfer" rather than "$17.3M"

## PARAGRAPH PACING

- Lead with the recommendation
- Quantify the impact in the second sentence
- Explain mechanic briefly in third sentence
- Identify what client needs to do or who handles what in fourth sentence
- Move on

Plans where each recommendation runs 3-5 paragraphs of justification become unreadable. The KB structure (separate file per rec) lets the plan output be tight; depth lives in the KB and supports the plan, not in the plan body.
