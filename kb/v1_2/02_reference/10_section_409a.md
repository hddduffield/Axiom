# §409A Non-Qualified Deferred Compensation — Reference

## SCOPE

§409A applies to non-qualified deferred compensation arrangements between employer and employee/contractor:
- SERPs (REC-SUC-001)
- Phantom equity (REC-SUC-005)
- SARs (REC-SUC-006) [though some structurally outside §409A]
- Stay bonuses if deferred (REC-SUC-002)
- Long-term incentive plans
- Settlement payments deferred

Does NOT apply to:
- Qualified plans (401(k), pension, etc. — separately regulated)
- Stock options (incentive or non-qual; separately regulated under §83 / §422)
- Profits interests (REC-SUC-007 — partnership treatment)
- Short-term deferrals (paid within 2.5 months of year vesting)

## KEY REQUIREMENTS

### Initial deferral elections
- Must be made BEFORE the year services are performed
- Special first-year rule for new participant (within 30 days of becoming eligible)
- Cannot be modified after deadline

### Distribution timing
Permitted distribution events ONLY:
1. Separation from service
2. Death
3. Disability (§409A definition)
4. Specified time / fixed schedule
5. Change in control (limited)
6. Unforeseeable emergency (limited)

### 6-month delay for "specified employees"
Public-company specified employees (top officers): distributions on separation must be delayed 6 months.

### No acceleration
Cannot accelerate payment of deferred amounts (with limited exceptions).

### No subsequent deferrals
Once elected, cannot defer further (with limited exceptions).

## VIOLATION CONSEQUENCES

Severe:
- All deferred amounts under that plan + similar plans of same employee are immediately taxable
- 20% additional federal tax (penalty)
- Interest on underpayment computed back to deferral year

This is why §409A compliance is non-negotiable.

## "SHORT-TERM DEFERRAL" EXEMPTION

Compensation paid by the later of (i) 2.5 months after employer's tax year ending in which substantial risk of forfeiture lapses, or (ii) 2.5 months after employee's tax year. Most stay bonuses and immediate-payment retention designs structured to fit.

## STRUCTURAL OPTIONS THAT AVOID §409A

- Profits interests (partnership context)
- Restricted stock / RSUs paid promptly on vesting
- Stock options at fair market value strike (separately regulated under §422 / §83)
- Short-term deferrals (paid within 2.5 months)

## RABBI TRUST

Structure where employer "trustee" holds informal funding for NQDC promises. Assets are still general creditors of employer (unfunded for ERISA / §409A); but provides assurance to executive against employer change of mind.

## COORDINATION WITH §457

Government and tax-exempt employer NQDC has additional §457(b) and §457(f) rules — different from §409A. Not applicable to PSA's typical for-profit business clients.
