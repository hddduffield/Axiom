# §469 Passive Activity Rules — Reference

## CORE MECHANICS

Losses from "passive activity" deductible only against passive income; excess suspended and carried forward until disposition or future passive income.

## PASSIVE ACTIVITY DEFINITION

- Any rental activity (real estate rentals presumptively passive — but see exceptions)
- Any trade or business in which taxpayer does not materially participate

## MATERIAL PARTICIPATION TESTS (anyone passes if any)

1. >500 hours/year
2. Substantially all of activity (regardless of total hours)
3. >100 hours and at least as many as any other person
4. Significant participation activity (>100 hours; aggregate)
5. Material in 5 of last 10 years
6. Personal service activity (any 3 prior years)
7. Facts and circumstances (>100 hours and management)

## REAL ESTATE PROFESSIONAL EXCEPTION

Material participant qualifying as real estate professional:
- >50% of personal services in real property trades/businesses
- >750 hours in real property trades/businesses

For real estate professionals, rental activities are NOT presumptively passive — material participation determines.

## §469 GROUPING ELECTION

Multiple activities can be grouped if they form an "appropriate economic unit." Once grouped, treated as one activity for material participation purposes.

**Common application:** owner of an operating business + owner of real estate rented to that business can group, making the rental NOT passive (avoiding suspended losses on the rental and supporting QBI deduction).

## §469 RECAPTURE EVENTS

- Disposition of activity (including in fully taxable transaction)
- Death of taxpayer (PALs deductible up to step-up)

## $25,000 EXCEPTION

Active participation real estate rental: up to $25,000 of losses deductible against non-passive income, phased out from $100K-$150K AGI. Not relevant for HNW PSA clients.
