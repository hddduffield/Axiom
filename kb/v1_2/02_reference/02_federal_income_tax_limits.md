## §401(a)(17) COMPENSATION LIMIT

- **2026: $360,000** (was $350,000 in 2025)
- Caps the amount of compensation taken into account for qualified plan purposes (deferrals, profit-sharing, DB benefit calc, etc.)
- Critical for cash-balance plan maximum-benefit calculations

## §415 LIMITS

### §415(c) — DC Plan Annual Addition Limit
- **2026: $72,000** (was $70,000 in 2025)
- This is the cap on TOTAL annual additions (employee deferral + employer match + profit sharing + after-tax) to a defined contribution plan, EXCLUDING catch-up contributions
- Catch-up (50+): adds $8,000 → effective DC max $80,000 for 50+ participants
- Super catch-up (60–63): adds $11,250 → effective DC max $83,250 for 60–63 participants
- This is the upper bound for mega-backdoor Roth capacity

### §415(b) — DB Plan Annual Benefit Limit
- **2026: $290,000** (was $280,000 in 2025)
- Maximum life-annuity-equivalent benefit a defined-benefit plan can provide at age 65
- Adjusted by actuary for retirement age, payment form, service years
- Drives cash-balance plan maximum contribution math

## SECURE 2.0 ROTH CATCH-UP MANDATE (NEW IN 2026)

- **Threshold:** prior-year (2025) FICA wages > **$150,000**
- **Effect:** for 2026 catch-up contributions, the contribution MUST be Roth (after-tax) for affected individuals
- **Plan requirement:** plan must offer Roth contributions; if not, affected individual cannot make catch-up contributions at all in that plan
- **Affected plans:** 401(k), 403(b), governmental 457(b)
- **Not affected:** IRAs, SIMPLE plans (have separate rules)
- **Roth catch-up wage threshold (going forward):** indexed; 2025 threshold = $150,000 used to determine 2026 status
- Citation: IRC §414(v)(7); SECURE 2.0 §603

## QBI (§199A) — 2026 (POST-OBBBA, NOW PERMANENT)

- 20% deduction on qualified pass-through business income
- **Phase-in thresholds (OBBBA-raised):**
  - **MFJ: $150,000** (was $100,000 pre-OBBBA)
  - **Single: $75,000**
- Above phase-in thresholds, SSTB businesses lose deduction; non-SSTB businesses face W-2-wage and qualified-property limits
- **Minimum deduction:** $400 floor for taxpayers with at least $1,000 of QBI (OBBBA-added)
- Citation: IRC §199A; OBBBA permanence

See `03_qbi_section_199a.md` for full detail.

## ESTIMATED TAX SAFE HARBORS (NOT 2026-SPECIFIC)

To avoid underpayment penalty:
- Pay 90% of current-year tax, OR
- Pay 100% of prior-year tax (if prior-year AGI ≤ $150,000)
- Pay **110%** of prior-year tax (if prior-year AGI > $150,000)

Applicable to most PSA clients — the 110% rule applies, since AGI is typically well above $150K.
