# Glossary Terms — Source-of-Truth List

Stage 4's deterministic glossary builder uses this file as its term-and-definition source. Every plan generated includes a glossary subset based on which terms are used in plan prose. Terms are added when new strategies introduce new vocabulary.

Schema per entry:
- **term**: the glossary entry as it appears in plan prose
- **acronym**: the abbreviation (if applicable)
- **plain_english_definition**: 1–3 sentences, accessible to a sophisticated client without finance/legal training

## Terms

### BOE — Business Overhead Expense

Disability insurance that reimburses the business for fixed operating costs while the owner is disabled.

### COLI — Corporate-Owned Life Insurance

Life insurance owned by the business on key employees, often used to informally fund deferred compensation arrangements.

### DAF — Donor-Advised Fund

A charitable account where you take the deduction at funding but recommend grants to charities over time. The simplest charitable vehicle.

### DI — Disability Insurance

Replaces a portion of income (typically 60–70%) if you cannot work due to illness or injury.

### F-Reorg — Section 368(a)(1)(F) Reorganization

A tax-free corporate restructuring that creates a new entity layered above an existing one — useful for holdco structuring and §1202 stock planning.

### GRAT — Grantor Retained Annuity Trust

A trust that pays you back the full value of what you contribute (plus an IRS-set interest rate) over a set term. Any growth above that rate passes to your heirs free of gift tax.

### ILIT — Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust

A trust that owns a life insurance policy so that proceeds pass outside the insured's taxable estate.

### IDGT — Intentionally Defective Grantor Trust

A trust structured so the grantor pays the income tax on the trust's earnings (an additional gift-tax-free transfer) but the assets are outside the grantor's estate. "Defective" is a term of art — it works exactly as intended.

### LOI — Letter of Intent

Non-binding outline of deal terms, typically signed before due diligence in a transaction.

### PTET — Pass-Through Entity Tax

An election that pays state tax at the entity level rather than the individual level — bypassing the $10,000 SALT cap on personal returns.

### QBID — Qualified Business Income Deduction

A 20% deduction on qualified pass-through business income, subject to income and industry limits.

### QofE — Quality of Earnings

A detailed analysis of a business's earnings done by an independent firm, typically commissioned ahead of a transaction.

### SERP — Supplemental Executive Retirement Plan

A non-qualified retirement plan funded by the employer for select key employees, typically with vesting schedules to encourage retention.

### §1202 — IRC Section 1202

Allows up to 100% federal tax exclusion on the sale of qualified small business stock (QSBS) held more than 5 years, subject to limits. Generally requires C-Corp structure.

### §469 — IRC Section 469 (Passive Activity Rules)

Generally disallows passive losses (like rental real estate) from offsetting active business income — but a grouping election can change that.

### UTMA / UGMA — Uniform Transfers / Gifts to Minors Act

Custodial accounts that allow assets to be held for a minor; control transfers to the child at age of majority (21 in Georgia).
