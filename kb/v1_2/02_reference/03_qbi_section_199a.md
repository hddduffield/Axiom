# QBI / §199A — Reference

**Status post-OBBBA:** PERMANENT. Was scheduled to expire 12/31/2025; OBBBA made permanent.

## CORE MECHANICS

20% deduction on qualified business income from pass-through entity (S-Corp, LLC, partnership, sole proprietorship).

**2026 phase-in thresholds (OBBBA-raised):**
- MFJ: $150,000 (was lower under TCJA)
- Single: $75,000

**Above thresholds:** SSTB businesses lose deduction; non-SSTB face W-2 wage and qualified property limits.

**Below thresholds:** full 20% deduction available regardless of business type.

**Minimum deduction:** $400 for taxpayers with at least $1,000 of QBI (OBBBA-added).

## SSTB DETERMINATION

Specified Service Trades or Businesses ineligible above the phase-out ceiling:
- Health, law, accounting, actuarial science
- Performing arts, consulting, athletics
- Financial services, brokerage services
- Investment management
- Any business where principal asset is reputation/skill of one or more employees/owners

**Critical:** "Health" is broad — includes physicians but excludes some health-adjacent businesses (medical device sales). Specific facts matter.

## W-2 WAGE LIMIT (NON-SSTB ABOVE PHASE-IN)

Above thresholds, non-SSTB deduction limited to greater of:
- 50% of W-2 wages paid by the qualified business, OR
- 25% of W-2 wages + 2.5% of unadjusted basis in qualified property

This is why **W-2 / K-1 mix optimization (REC-TAX-002)** matters: too low W-2 caps deduction; too high W-2 wastes deduction.

## QUALIFIED PROPERTY

Tangible property used in the qualified trade or business that hasn't reached the end of its depreciable period (longer of 10 years or normal recovery period). Important for capital-intensive businesses.

## AGGREGATION

Multiple qualified businesses can be aggregated for §199A purposes if they meet ownership and similarity tests. Election permanent once made.

## REPORTING

- Form 8995 (simplified) — below phase-in
- Form 8995-A — above phase-in or SSTB
- K-1 reports QBI components (line 17 codes)
