# Chapter 14 Special Valuation Rules — Reference

## SCOPE

IRC §§ 2701-2704 govern special valuation rules in family transactions. Aimed at preventing valuation games in transfers between family members.

## §2701 — RECAPITALIZATIONS

Restrictions on transfers of equity interests in family-controlled entities where transferor retains certain "applicable retained interests" (preferred or carried interests).

**General rule:** retained interest valued at zero unless it is a "qualified payment" (cumulative dividend at fixed/ascertainable rate).

**Practical impact on PSA's lane:** voting/non-voting recap (REC-ENT-003) generally NOT a §2701 issue because both classes are common (not preferred). Issue arises with preferred-equity recapitalizations (less common in PSA's mid-market lane).

## §2702 — TRANSFERS WITH RETAINED INTERESTS (GRAT)

When transferor retains an interest in trust and transfers remainder to family member, retained interest valued at zero UNLESS it's a "qualified annuity interest" or "qualified unitrust interest."

**Qualified annuity interest:** fixed dollar amount or fixed percentage of initial fair market value, paid at least annually for fixed term. **The basis for GRAT.**

**Walton GRAT:** Walton v. Commissioner (2000) confirmed annuity continuing for lesser of term or grantor's life is fully qualified. Allows zero-gift GRAT when annuity properly sized to §7520.

## §2703 — APPLICABLE RESTRICTIONS

Restrictions on the right to use property or its sale price are disregarded for valuation UNLESS:
- Bona fide business arrangement
- Not a device to transfer to family for less than full consideration
- Comparable to similar arrangements among unrelated parties

**Practical impact:** family-only transfer restrictions in operating agreements may not support discounts; market-tested restrictions in arm's-length agreements respected.

## §2704 — LAPSING RIGHTS AND APPLICABLE RESTRICTIONS

§2704(a): Lapsing voting/liquidation rights treated as transfers (anti-circumvention).

§2704(b): "Applicable restrictions" on liquidation that are MORE restrictive than state law default are disregarded for valuation. Restrictions that mirror state law default ARE respected.

**Withdrawn 2016 proposed regulations:** would have severely limited valuation discounts (especially family-only restrictions). **Withdrawn under prior administration; OBBBA did NOT reinstate.** Current law allows traditional discount approaches subject to qualified appraisal.

## DISCOUNT TYPICAL RANGES

Per qualified appraisal:
- Lack of marketability (DLOM): 15%-35%
- Lack of control (minority interest): 10%-25%
- Combined: 25%-40% defensible

Aggressive (>40%) requires exceptional facts.

**Defensibility = qualified appraisal.** No appraisal = no defensible discount.
