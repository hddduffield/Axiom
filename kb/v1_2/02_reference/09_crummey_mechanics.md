# Crummey Power Mechanics — Reference

## CORE MECHANIC

A "Crummey power" is a beneficiary's right to withdraw contributions to a trust for a defined demand period (typically 30-60 days). The withdrawal right makes the gift a "present interest" qualifying for the annual exclusion under §2503(b).

Origin: Crummey v. Commissioner, 397 F.2d 82 (9th Cir. 1968).

## REQUIREMENTS FOR ANNUAL EXCLUSION QUALIFICATION

1. **Real withdrawal right** — beneficiary must have actual ability to demand and receive the contribution
2. **Notice** — beneficiary must receive written notice of contribution and withdrawal right
3. **Reasonable demand period** — typically 30-60 days (less may be challenged)
4. **Trustee's good faith** — trustee must honor demands if made

## DOCUMENTATION

For each Crummey contribution:
- Trustee letter to beneficiary stating: amount of contribution, withdrawal right, deadline
- Acknowledgment of receipt (return receipt or signed acknowledgment)
- File copy retained
- After demand period: contribution deemed completed gift to trust

## LAPSE PROBLEMS — 5/$5,000 RULE

Lapse of withdrawal right beyond the greater of $5,000 or 5% of trust corpus is itself a taxable gift by the beneficiary back to the trust (general power of appointment under IRC §2514).

**Example:** Beneficiary's withdrawal right of $19,000 over a $50,000 trust corpus:
- 5% × $50,000 = $2,500
- Greater of $5,000 or 5%: $5,000
- $19,000 - $5,000 = $14,000 lapse over the limit → taxable gift by beneficiary

## HANGING CRUMMEY POWER

Solution to lapse problem: the unused portion of the withdrawal right does NOT lapse but "hangs" indefinitely until lapse limit clears.

**Mechanic:** beneficiary's withdrawal right remains exercisable for cumulative unused amounts up to the 5/$5,000 limit per year. Each year that beneficiary doesn't withdraw, $5,000 (or 5%) of past hanging power lapses; remainder carries.

Used in most modern ILIT and similar Crummey-trust structures.

## ADMINISTRATIVE CADENCE

- Contribution to trust
- Trustee sends Crummey notices to all beneficiaries with hanging or current withdrawal right
- Beneficiaries' demand period (typically 30-60 days)
- Demand period expires; contribution becomes completed gift
- Documentation filed in trust records

## COMMON ERRORS

- **No notice given:** contribution does not qualify as present interest; full annual exclusion lost
- **Stale notices:** form letters not updated; beneficiary status changed (e.g., minor became adult, marriage, divorce)
- **Trustee bad faith:** Crummey power not respected if asked
- **Inadequate notice period:** less than reasonable
- **Failure to track hanging power:** lapse problems compound
- **Beneficiary actually exercises:** trust loses funding for the year (rare in practice; family understanding)

## COORDINATION WITH ANNUAL EXCLUSION

Crummey contributions count against the donor's annual exclusion ($19,000 in 2026 per donor per donee). Multiple beneficiaries multiply: with 3 children + 5 grandchildren = 8 beneficiaries × $19,000 = $152,000 of Crummey-qualifying gifts annually per donor (or $304,000 if both spouses gift-split).
