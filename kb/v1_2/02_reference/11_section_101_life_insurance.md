# §101 Life Insurance Tax Treatment — Reference

## §101(a) — DEATH BENEFIT TAX-FREE

Death benefit proceeds from life insurance policy generally excluded from beneficiary's gross income.

**Critical exceptions:**

### Transfer-for-value rule (§101(a)(2))
If policy was transferred for valuable consideration to anyone other than:
- The insured
- A partner of the insured
- A partnership in which the insured is a partner
- A corporation in which the insured is a shareholder or officer
...then death benefit becomes taxable income.

**Common violation:** cross-purchase buy/sell where ownership shifts among owners; if Owner A transfers a policy on Owner B to Owner C for value, transfer-for-value triggers.

**Safe harbors:** intra-group transfers (insured, insured's partnership, insured's corporation); transfers between certain related parties.

### §101(j) — Employer-Owned Life Insurance (EOLI)
Death benefit from policy:
- Owned by employer or related entity
- Insured is employee, director, officer, or 5%+ owner

...is fully TAXABLE to employer **unless** notice-and-consent satisfied:
1. Notice in writing to insured BEFORE policy issuance
2. Insured consents in writing
3. Notice explains: employer will be policyowner; max face amount; insured's continued status; any rights to specified percentage of death benefit

Plus annual reporting via **Form 8925** with corporate return.

**Critical for:** key person life insurance (REC-RSK-007), redemption-based buy/sell (REC-RSK-002), SERPs informally funded with COLI (REC-SUC-001).

## §101(g) — ACCELERATED DEATH BENEFITS

Pre-death payments to terminally ill or chronically ill insured may be excluded.

## §101(j)(2)(B) — STATUTORY EXCEPTIONS

Exceptions to §101(j) inclusion (limited):
- Death benefit paid to family member of insured
- Death benefit paid to designated beneficiary other than employer
- Death benefit used to fund buy/sell agreement involving family

## TYPICAL OPERATING STRUCTURE

Buy/sell with cross-purchase:
- §101(a) tax-free (insured-as-owner of others' policies typical)
- Watch transfer-for-value if ownership shifts

Buy/sell with redemption (corp as owner):
- §101(j) applies
- Notice-and-consent mandatory
- Form 8925 annually
- §2042 incidents-of-ownership consideration (typically OK because insured doesn't own)

ILIT:
- §101(a) clean (ILIT as owner; insured separate party; not employer)
- §2042 satisfied (insured has no incidents of ownership in ILIT-owned policy)
- §2035 3-year lookback if existing personally-owned policy transferred to ILIT and insured dies within 3 years

## KEY DOCUMENTATION FOR §101(j)

- Notice template signed by each insured BEFORE policy issuance
- Annual Form 8925 with corporate return
- Documentation that notice was given and consent received pre-issuance
- File copies retained
