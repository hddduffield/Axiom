# OBBBA — One Big Beautiful Bill Act (P.L. 119-21)

**Signed into law:** July 4, 2025
**Effective:** Various — see each provision
**Significance:** Largest tax legislation since TCJA (2017). The OBBBA permanently extends and expands many TCJA-era provisions and modifies several. Multiple changes affect every recommendation in this Knowledge Base.

The Holloway plan was drafted referencing pre-OBBBA numbers in some places. The corrections in this file supersede anything in older plan deliveries.

---

## ESTATE & GIFT TAX

### Lifetime Exemption — INCREASED
- **2025:** $13.99M individual / $27.98M couple
- **2026 (OBBBA):** **$15M individual / $30M couple**
- **Indexing:** Continues to be inflation-adjusted annually
- **Sunset:** OBBBA REMOVED the prior 2026 sunset; the higher exemption is now "permanent" (subject to future legislation)
- **Citation:** OBBBA amended IRC §2010(c)(3)
- **Practical impact:** A married couple has $2.02M more transfer capacity than they had in 2025. Couples who exhausted prior exemption can use this incremental amount.

### Annual Gift Exclusion — UNCHANGED
- **2026:** $19,000 per donor per donee (gift-splitting → $38,000 per recipient for couples)
- **Non-citizen spouse exclusion:** $194,000 in 2026
- **Citation:** IRC §2503(b)

### GST Exemption — TRACKS ESTATE/GIFT
- Same $15M/$30M for 2026
- Same indexing
- Citation: IRC §2631

## INCOME TAX — RATES & STRUCTURE

### Top Federal Rate — UNCHANGED at 37%
- TCJA top rate of 37% extended permanently
- Without OBBBA, would have reverted to 39.6% post-2025

### Standard Deduction — INCREASED
- **2026 MFJ:** $32,200
- **2026 Single/HOH:** $16,100

### SALT Cap — INCREASED with PHASE-OUT
- **Pre-OBBBA:** $10,000 cap
- **2026 OBBBA:** **$40,000 cap** (MFJ; $20,000 if MFS)
- **Phase-out:** Cap reduced by 30 cents per $1 of AGI over $500,000 (MFJ) / $250,000 (MFS)
  - At MFJ AGI of $605,000+, cap floor of $10,000 reapplies
- **Increment:** Cap increases by 1% per year 2027–2029
- **Sunset:** Cap drops back to $10,000 ($5,000 MFS) **permanently in 2030**
- **Citation:** OBBBA amended IRC §164(b)(6)
- **Practical impact:** PTET still beneficial above $500K AGI because phase-out reduces SALT cap usability; below $500K AGI with state/local taxes under $40K, PTET may now be neutral or unhelpful

### QBI Deduction (§199A) — MADE PERMANENT
- 20% deduction continues
- Phase-in thresholds raised: $75,000 single / **$150,000 MFJ** (was lower under TCJA)
- Minimum $400 deduction floor for taxpayers with at least $1,000 of QBI
- Was scheduled to expire end of 2025; **now permanent under OBBBA**
- Citation: OBBBA made IRC §199A permanent

## DEPRECIATION — PERMANENT 100% BONUS

### Bonus Depreciation — RESTORED & PERMANENT
- **Pre-OBBBA:** Phase-down schedule — 60% (2024), 40% (2025), 20% (2026), 0% (2027)
- **OBBBA:** **100% bonus depreciation permanent** for property acquired after **January 19, 2025** (the OBBBA acquisition date cutoff)
- Property acquired before Jan 20, 2025: subject to original phase-down (40% if placed in service in 2025)
- Election available to take 40% (60% for long-production-period property) instead of 100% in first year ending after 1/19/25
- **Citation:** OBBBA amended IRC §168(k); IRS Notice 2026-11 (interim guidance, Jan 14, 2026)
- **Practical impact:** Cost segregation studies are dramatically more powerful again. Major change for real-estate-owning clients.

### Section 179 Limits — INCREASED
- Annual Section 179 cap raised significantly under OBBBA
- Particularly valuable for property NOT eligible for bonus depreciation (e.g., roofs, HVAC, fire protection systems)
- More reliable than bonus for state-conformity purposes (most states conform to §179 even when they don't conform to bonus depreciation)
- [VERIFY 2026 — confirm current §179 cap]

### Section 168(n) — NEW
- 100% first-year expensing for nonresidential real property used in **manufacturing, production, or refining activities**
- 39-year property structures normally; this provision allows full expensing
- **Excluded:** office space, administrative areas, R&D, software development, sales areas, lodging, parking
- [VERIFY 2026 — confirm definition and limits]

## §1202 QSBS — DRAMATICALLY EXPANDED

This is one of the most significant OBBBA changes for the firm's lane. Major opportunity for pre-exit business owners considering C-Corp restructuring.

### Holding Period — TIERED
For QSBS issued **after July 4, 2025**:

| Holding Period | Exclusion |
|---|---|
| 3+ years | **50%** |
| 4+ years | **75%** |
| 5+ years | **100%** |

QSBS issued **on or before July 4, 2025**: original 5-year-for-100% rule applies.

**Note:** Unexcluded gain (50% remaining at 3 yrs, 25% at 4 yrs) is taxed at **28% LTCG rate** plus NIIT — not the standard 15%/20% rates.

### Per-Issuer Exclusion Cap — INCREASED
- **Pre-OBBBA:** $10M or 10× basis (whichever greater)
- **OBBBA (post-7/4/25 issuance):** **$15M** or 10× basis (whichever greater)
- $15M cap **inflation-indexed beginning 2027**
- 10× basis cap unchanged but more potent due to higher gross-asset threshold

### Aggregate Gross Asset Test — INCREASED
- **Pre-OBBBA:** $50M cap on issuer's gross assets (for stock to qualify)
- **OBBBA (post-7/4/25 issuance):** **$75M** cap
- Inflation-indexed beginning 2027
- **Practical impact:** Many growth-stage businesses now qualify that would have been disqualified at $50M

### Section 1045 Rollover — UNCHANGED
- Sale of QSBS held >6 months can be rolled into new QSBS within 60 days
- Holding period tacks
- Useful when investor sells before 5-year (or 3/4/5-year) hold

### State Conformity — HEAVILY VARIES
- **Conforming:** most states
- **Non-conforming (taxable at state level despite federal exclusion):** Alabama, California, Mississippi, New Jersey, Pennsylvania
- **Partial:** Hawaii, Massachusetts
- **Georgia:** [VERIFY 2026 — confirm current GA conformity]
- Planning consideration: irrevocable non-grantor trust in no-income-tax state (DE, NV, SD, WY, AK) can avoid state tax on QSBS gain

### Citation
- IRC §1202 (as amended by OBBBA)
- IRC §1045 (rollover)

## SECURE 2.0 ROTH CATCH-UP — EFFECTIVE 2026

This is a SECURE 2.0 provision (not OBBBA), but it takes effect in 2026 and affects high earners.

- **Rule:** Catch-up contributions to 401(k), 403(b), and governmental 457(b) plans **MUST be Roth (after-tax)** for participants whose **prior-year FICA wages exceed $150,000** (the 2025 threshold)
- **Threshold:** $150,000 in 2025 wages → Roth catch-up required in 2026; threshold indexed annually
- **Plan must support Roth:** if the plan doesn't offer Roth, high earners cannot make catch-up contributions at all
- **Citation:** SECURE 2.0 §603, codified at IRC §414(v)(7)
- **Practical impact:** PSA must verify with each client's plan provider that Roth contributions are supported. If not, plan amendment may be required to preserve catch-up capability for the owner. This is now a default item for any client at deferral cap with FICA wages >$150K in 2025.

## OTHER OBBBA PROVISIONS RELEVANT TO PSA'S LANE

### 1099 Reporting Thresholds — INCREASED
- 1099-NEC: threshold raised from $600 to **$2,000** starting 2026
- 1099-K (third-party payment): now requires gross sales >$20,000 AND >200 transactions
- Citation: OBBBA amended IRC §6041

### Employer-Provided Childcare Credit — EXPANDED
- 2026: 40% of eligible costs (up from 25%)
- Maximum credit: $500,000
- Small business: 50% of eligible costs, max $600,000
- Useful for owner-clients who provide childcare benefits

### Estate Tax Generation-Skipping — UNCHANGED MECHANICS
- 40% top rate
- $15M/$30M exemption (combined estate/gift/GST)

### §139 Disaster-Relief Payments — STILL AVAILABLE
- Tax-free payments to employees during qualified disasters
- Useful in hurricane / pandemic / federally-declared-disaster contexts
- Citation: IRC §139

## WHAT IS NOT IN OBBBA (still on the table)

- No anti-GRAT legislation enacted (the "10-year minimum term" / "minimum gift value > 0" proposals did NOT pass)
- No anti-IDGT-sale legislation enacted
- §2704 valuation discount limits NOT changed (proposed regulations from 2016 remain withdrawn)
- §1014 basis-step-up at death NOT modified
- Step-up still applies; community-property-state full step-up still applies
- Carried interest treatment NOT changed

## ACTION ITEMS FOR THE GENERATOR

When generating any plan in 2026:

1. **Always cite OBBBA changes by their post-OBBBA values** for: estate/gift exemption, §1202 thresholds, bonus depreciation availability, QBI permanence, SALT cap and phase-out
2. **For pre-OBBBA-issued QSBS,** apply original §1202 rules; for post-7/4/25 issuance, apply OBBBA tiered structure
3. **For PTET,** still recommend for clients with AGI >$500K (where SALT cap phases out toward $10K floor); for clients between $40K SALT and $500K AGI, model both paths
4. **For SECURE 2.0 Roth catch-up,** confirm plan supports Roth before recommending catch-up contributions for high earners; flag plan amendment if not
5. **For bonus depreciation,** model 100% (post 1/19/25 acquisition) — this dramatically improves cost-seg recommendation impact
