# Grantor Trust Rules — Reference

## CORE CONCEPT

A "grantor trust" is one where the grantor is treated as the OWNER for income tax purposes. Trust income, deductions, and credits flow through to grantor's personal tax return regardless of actual distributions.

Codified at IRC §§ 671-679.

## WHY THIS MATTERS

Grantor-trust treatment is the foundation of:
- IDGT (REC-EST-008) — grantor pays income tax; trust assets grow free of income tax burden; effectively a "tax-free gift" of the income tax dollars
- Sale to grantor trust (Rev. Rul. 85-13) — sale not recognized for income tax because seller and buyer are treated as same person
- Many ILITs are grantor-trust for income tax (typically only marginal income tax exposure)

## TRIGGERS FOR GRANTOR TRUST STATUS

### Reversionary interest (§673)
Grantor retains reversionary interest valued at >5% at trust creation. Rare in modern planning.

### Power to control beneficial enjoyment (§674)
Grantor retains power to control who gets income/principal. Various exceptions for independent trustees.

### Administrative powers (§675) — **WORKHORSE**
- §675(4)(C) — power to substitute assets of equivalent value, exercisable in non-fiduciary capacity. **THE STANDARD IDGT TRIGGER.** Donor retains right to swap trust assets for personal assets of equal value. Triggers grantor trust for income tax but NOT §2036/§2038 estate inclusion.

### Power to revoke (§676)
Power to revoke makes trust grantor for income (and revocable for estate purposes — usually used for revocable living trust, not for IDGT).

### Income for benefit of grantor (§677)
Income may be paid to grantor or grantor's spouse without consent of adverse party.

### Foreign trust (§679)
Foreign trust with US grantor and US beneficiary.

## §675(4)(C) — THE WORKHORSE PROVISION

The substitute power is the gold standard for IDGT planning because:
- Triggers grantor trust status (income tax mechanism)
- Does NOT trigger §2036/§2038 (estate inclusion)
- Confirmed by Rev. Rul. 2008-22 and Rev. Rul. 2011-28

Power must be exercisable in **non-fiduciary capacity** — typically held by grantor personally, not as trustee.

## TURNING OFF GRANTOR STATUS

Grantor status can be turned off by releasing the triggering power (e.g., releasing the substitute power). Sometimes done at transition events (sale of trust to family member, etc.).

## INCOME TAX MECHANICS WHILE GRANTOR

- Trust income reported on grantor's 1040
- Trust uses grantor's SSN (not its own EIN, in many cases)
- Trust may file informational Form 1041 with grantor letter
- Grantor pays income tax from personal funds — preserves trust corpus

## TURN-OFF / TURN-ON PLANNING

Some trusts are designed to switch grantor status at events:
- IDGT structures often turn off at sale of business (income from business no longer flows to grantor)
- Tax-burden management
