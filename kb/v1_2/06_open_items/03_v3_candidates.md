## TIER 3 DEFERRED FROM v1.2 CALIBRATION-FIX PASS

These items emerged from the calibration test against the Holloway exemplar but were explicitly deferred during the v1.2 surgical-fix session. Each requires firm-policy input, fact-review template revision, or generator-architecture decisions that fall outside the KB's surgical scope.

### 1. PTET federal savings calculation discrepancy
**Status:** Pending Will's input.

The KB's PTET savings calculation (REC-TAX-001) produces ~$61K of federal savings for the Holloway facts; the Holloway plan claims $148K. The KB calculation reconciles arithmetically under post-OBBBA SALT-cap-with-phase-out mechanics: ($176,460 GA tax shifted to entity − $10,000 SALT floor at high AGI) × 37% federal marginal = $61,590. The Holloway plan's $148K does not reconcile under any combination of rate × marginal × cap I can derive.

**Possible explanations:**
- Holloway plan figure was directional rather than precisely computable
- Holloway plan included optimization beyond just PTET (e.g., paired with W-2/K-1 mix)
- Holloway plan was drafted under different SALT-cap assumptions
- Holloway plan figure is wrong

**Decision needed:** Confirm with Will whether the KB calculation is the authoritative number going forward; if so, the Holloway plan's $148K figure should be treated as a directional estimate, not a calibration target.

### 2. Fact Review gap — co-owner insurability
**Status:** Pending FR template revision.

Cross-purchase buy/sell (REC-RSK-001) requires both owners to be insurable. The Fact Review template captures the primary owner's health/insurability (FR.2.1.health_status) but does not capture the co-owner's. The trigger condition "At least ONE owner insurable" passes on the primary alone, which is insufficient for the actual mechanism.

**Decision needed:** Add to FR §3.2 (Ownership table) a column or sub-row for "Health / Insurability" for each owner, or capture co-owner insurability in a new sub-section. This is a Fact Review template change, not a KB recommendation change.

### 3. Fact Review gap — mortgage covenants on business real estate
**Status:** Pending FR template revision.

REC-ENT-001 has a hard disqualifier for "Mortgage covenants prohibit transfer." The Fact Review template (FR §3.6) captures the property's location, holding entity, and estimated value, but does not capture mortgage status or covenants. The disqualifier cannot be evaluated from the FR alone.

**Decision needed:** Add to FR §3.6 (Real Estate Held by or Used by the Business): "Mortgage on this property: Y/N" and "If Y, transfer restrictions: Y/N/Unknown."

### 4. "Why this sequence matters" attribution / generator ownership
**Status:** Deferred until Plan Generator chat (Chat 04) is opened.

The Holloway plan ends Section 1 (Entity & Real Estate Structure) with a "Why this sequence matters" closer — a paragraph that ties the recommendations together with explicit sequencing rationale. The KB's REC-ENT-002 plan output template includes commented-out scaffolding for this closer, but it's unclear whether the closer should be:
- Generated by REC-ENT-002's template (current scaffolding)
- Generated by the sequencing files in `03_sequencing/`
- Generated by the Plan Generator separately when it detects a sequenced restructuring set

**Decision needed:** Architectural decision belongs in Chat 04 (Plan Generator). Until then, the scaffolding in REC-ENT-002 remains; the generator can choose to consume or override.
