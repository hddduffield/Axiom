# Section-Specific Disclosures

Disclosures that attach to specific sections or recommendations.

## INVESTMENT SECTION

> Investment recommendations reflect PSA Wealth's view based on current portfolio analysis, the client's stated risk tolerance, and current market conditions. Securities offered through MML Investors Services, LLC. Past performance does not guarantee future results.

## INSURANCE SECTION

> Insurance recommendations are based on illustrative pricing and standard underwriting; actual issue depends on underwriting outcomes including health, financial, and other factors. Insurance products are offered through MassMutual and other approved carriers.

## ESTATE PLANNING SECTION

> Estate planning strategies described require specific legal documentation drafted by an attorney; PSA Wealth coordinates planning but does not draft legal documents. Tax outcomes depend on then-current law at the time of any actual gift or transfer; current federal exemption ($15M / $30M MFJ as of 2026) and other parameters may change.

## TAX SECTION

> Tax strategies described require execution by your CPA, including filing of elections, returns, and other documents. Specific tax savings depend on individual circumstances and current law. PSA Wealth does not file tax returns or provide tax advice.

## ENTITY STRUCTURE SECTION

> Entity restructuring (F-Reorganization, recapitalization, real estate separation, etc.) requires execution by qualified counsel. Federal tax-free treatment under IRC §368 and similar provisions depends on strict adherence to statutory requirements; specialist counsel mandatory.

## RETIREMENT BENEFITS SECTION

> Qualified retirement plan structures (401(k), cash balance, profit-sharing) require ongoing administration by a qualified TPA / actuary. Annual nondiscrimination testing is required; failure can result in plan disqualification. SECURE 2.0 §603 (Roth catch-up requirement effective 2026) and other compliance items must be addressed timely.

## CHARITABLE PLANNING SECTION

> Charitable strategies (DAF, CRT, CLT, foundation) require formal documentation and ongoing administration. Charitable deductions are subject to AGI limits and substantiation requirements (IRC §170; qualified appraisals required for non-cash gifts >$5,000). Carryforward of unused deduction is 5 years.

## SPECIALTY SECTION

> Specialty strategies (PPLI, ESOP/§1042, NING/DING, structured charitable bailout) require specialist counsel and accept significant complexity. PSA Wealth coordinates but does not act as primary counsel for these structures.
