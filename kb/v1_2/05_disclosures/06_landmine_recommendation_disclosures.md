# Landmine Recommendation Disclosures

These disclosures attach specifically to "Landmine" recommendations. They appear when senior advisor opt-in has been authorized and the recommendation is included in the plan.

## REC-RSK-016 — 831(b) CAPTIVE INSURANCE COMPANY

> **Captive Insurance Disclosure**
>
> The 831(b) micro-captive structure has been on the IRS Dirty Dozen list for over a decade. The IRS has won the major Tax Court cases challenging these structures, including Avrahami v. Commissioner (2017), Reserve Mechanical (2018), Syzygy (2019), and Caylor (2021). IRS Notice 2016-66 designates 831(b) micro-captives as transactions of interest with mandatory disclosure on Form 8886.
>
> This recommendation is included only because senior advisor authorization has determined that the client's specific facts present a genuine risk-management need (rather than primarily a tax-saving objective) and the structure is being established with specialist captive counsel and qualified independent actuarial support.
>
> Audit defense readiness is assumed from inception. Implementation requires Form 8886 disclosure annually, conservative actuarial pricing of insured risks, claim handling consistent with real insurance, and investment of reserves in non-related-party assets. Failure on any of these dimensions exposes the structure to penalty regimes including 40% accuracy penalties.

## REC-CHR-011 — CONSERVATION EASEMENT

> **Conservation Easement Disclosure**
>
> Conservation easements have been an IRS enforcement priority for years. Syndicated conservation easements specifically appear on the IRS Dirty Dozen list (per IRS Notice 2017-10). Tax Court rulings against aggressive valuations have been consistent. SECURE 2.0 Act §605 capped deductions for partnership-held syndicated easements at 2.5× basis.
>
> This recommendation is included only because senior advisor authorization has determined that the client's specific facts (long-held land, documented ecological value, conservative appraisal methodology, reputable land trust, willingness to engage specialist counsel) present a genuine and defensible conservation transaction.
>
> Implementation requires: (1) qualified, USPAP-compliant appraisal with conservative before-and-after methodology; (2) Form 8283 with appraiser certification; (3) baseline documentation; (4) recorded easement document; (5) audit defense readiness from inception. Aggressive appraisal multiples (e.g., >4× basis) substantially increase audit and challenge risk.

## REC-RSK-013 — PRIVATE PLACEMENT LIFE INSURANCE (PPLI)

> **PPLI Disclosure**
>
> Private Placement Life Insurance involves placement with institutional carriers (often offshore) and substantial structural complexity. The structure is sensitive to investor control rules; if the policyholder retains too much investment discretion, the IRS may challenge the insurance characterization, with the result that gains become currently taxable. Christoffersen v. Commissioner and subsequent guidance (Rev. Rul. 2003-91) define the limits.
>
> PPLI requires: specialist counsel; qualified purchaser status; long-term commitment (10+ years); careful investment management to avoid investor control violations; FATCA reporting if offshore; and acceptance of substantial fees including mortality charges, M&E fees, and investment management fees.

## REC-SPC-002 — NING / DING TRUST

> **NING / DING Trust Disclosure**
>
> Incomplete-gift Non-Grantor (ING) trusts are subject to evolving state-level positions. California (FTB Notice 2018-01) and New York have adopted positions effectively neutralizing the structure for residents of those states. The structure depends on (a) successful avoidance of grantor trust treatment for income tax, (b) successful incomplete-gift treatment for gift tax, and (c) successful situs in a no-tax state.
>
> Specialist counsel is mandatory. Distribution committee composition is technical; inadequate independent member presence can fail the structure.
