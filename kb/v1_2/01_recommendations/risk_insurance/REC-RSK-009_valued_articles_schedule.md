## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - FR.has_high_net_worth == True
  - Client owns valuable personal property: jewelry, art, collectibles, watches, firearms, fine wine, instruments
  - Standard homeowner sublimits (typically $1.5K-$5K per item) are inadequate

DISQUALIFY if:
  - No valuables outside standard homeowner coverage
```

### Natural-language explanation
Standard homeowner policies cap coverage on jewelry, art, collectibles at low sublimits ($1.5K-$5K per item is common). HNW clients often own pieces well above these sublimits — engagement rings, art, watches, instruments, wine collections — and need scheduled coverage with appraisal-based valuation, no deductible, and broader perils (mysterious disappearance, breakage).

### Hard disqualifiers
- All valuables already scheduled
- Items below standard sublimits

## WHAT IT IS
Inland marine / personal articles policy that schedules specific items at appraised value. Coverage is "all-risk" (vs. named perils on standard homeowner), no deductible (typically), and pays full appraised value on covered loss. Items must be appraised; appraisals refreshed periodically (typically every 5 years for art/jewelry).

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
A $50K engagement ring, $100K watch, $250K painting, $300K wine collection — homeowner caps these at $1.5K-$5K each. Without scheduling, theft or loss leaves a major gap. Scheduled coverage with current appraisals is inexpensive (~1-2% of value annually) and pays full value.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Coverage equal to appraised value
- All-risk perils including mysterious disappearance, breakage
- No deductible (typical)

### Worked example
Items totaling $400K (engagement ring $80K, watch collection $120K, art $200K):
- Standard homeowner: ~$5K total
- Scheduled valued articles: $400K
- Annual premium: ~$4K-$8K (1-2% of value)
- Coverage gap closed: $395K

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Inventory valuables: jewelry, art, watches, collectibles, instruments, firearms, fine wine, china/silver
2. Obtain qualified appraisals (USPAP for art; GIA-credentialed for diamonds/jewelry)
3. P&C broker schedules items at appraised value
4. Periodic appraisal refresh (5 years standard for art and jewelry; market-based items may be more frequent)
5. Annual coverage review with new acquisitions

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Self-valuing items rather than getting qualified appraisals
- Failing to refresh appraisals as market values move
- Forgetting to add new acquisitions
- Confusing standard homeowner sublimits with adequate coverage

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- Appraisal cost: $250-$1500 per item depending on type
- Some items (rapidly-appreciating art, vintage watches) need annual appraisal refresh
- Storage requirements (safe, security system) may apply
