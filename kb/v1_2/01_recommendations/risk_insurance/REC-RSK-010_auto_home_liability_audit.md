# [REC-RSK-010] — Auto / Home Liability Limits Audit

## METADATA
- **ID:** REC-RSK-010
- **Status:** Active
- **Category:** Risk & Insurance
- **Engagement archetypes:** All
- **Plan section placement:** "Recommendations — Personal Risk"
- **Last verified:** April 2026

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if:
  - Annual review trigger (annually for active client; first plan delivery)
  - Recent purchase of new home, vehicle, or watercraft
  - Material change in driver roster (new teen driver, addition/removal of family member)
  - Purchase of rental property or short-term rental income generation

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Reviewed within last 12 months and no material change
```

### Natural-language explanation
Primary auto and home liability limits should be reviewed annually for adequacy and to ensure they meet umbrella underlying requirements. Common gaps: low BI/PD limits on auto, low personal liability on homeowner, missing coverages for non-standard properties or activities.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Recent comprehensive review with no triggering changes

## WHAT IT IS
Annual or event-driven review of:
- Auto: bodily injury, property damage, UM/UIM, comprehensive/collision deductibles
- Home: dwelling coverage, personal property, personal liability, medical payments, ordinance/law
- Secondary properties: separate dwelling and liability
- Watercraft, ATV, RV: separate policies or endorsements
- Short-term rental activity: special endorsement or policy needed

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Most clients underbuy primary liability and overbuy comprehensive/collision; gaps in non-standard coverage are common. Periodic audit catches and closes gaps before claims expose them.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Adequate liability limits (typical recommendation: $500K BI per person/$500K per accident on auto; $1M home liability)
- UM/UIM limits matching liability
- Underlying limits sufficient for umbrella to attach
- Replacement cost on dwelling (verified periodically)

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Pull declarations pages for all primary policies
2. Audit limits against underwriting needs and umbrella underlying requirements
3. Identify coverage gaps (rental property, watercraft, short-term rental, golf cart, etc.)
4. Review deductibles vs. comfort level
5. Coordinate with P&C broker for adjustments

## SEQUENCING DEPENDENCIES
- **COORDINATED WITH:** REC-RSK-008 (umbrella)

## DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST
- [ ] Annual policy review documented
- [ ] All vehicles and properties on appropriate policies
- [ ] Liability limits at or above $500K minimum (and matching umbrella underlying)
- [ ] UM/UIM at appropriate level

## COMMON MISTAKES
- State-minimum auto liability with HNW exposure
- Old dwelling coverage not updated to current replacement cost
- Watercraft or ATV not on any policy
- Short-term rental income not disclosed → coverage void at claim
- Teen driver added without limit upgrade

## CLIENT CONVERSATION FRAMING
> "Annual review of your auto and home policies — quick but important. We're checking that your primary liability is sufficient (especially for the umbrella to attach), that all properties and vehicles are correctly insured, and that nothing has fallen through the cracks (rental properties, new watercraft, teen drivers). Goes through your P&C broker."

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- HNW carriers (Chubb, AIG, Pure) often default to higher coverage limits than mass-market carriers; some clients are with mass-market carriers and need a switch
- Coastal property, log home, historic home, custom build — special-class carriers may be appropriate

## REFERENCES
- Standard ISO HO and PA policy forms

Verify all properties, vehicles, watercraft, and rental activity are correctly covered. Coordinate with your P&C broker; PSA reviews the resulting summary annually.
