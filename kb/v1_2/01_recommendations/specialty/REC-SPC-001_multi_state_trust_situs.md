### Natural-language explanation
Trust situs is a planning lever. States like Delaware, South Dakota, Nevada, Wyoming, and Alaska offer favorable trust laws (no state income tax on trust; flexible decanting; long perpetuities periods; strong asset protection). For long-duration trusts, situs choice can save significant state tax over decades.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Insufficient scale; cannot avoid taxing-state nexus

## WHAT IT IS
Decision and structure: which state's law governs the trust, where it is administered, who serves as trustee. Different states have different trust income taxation, asset protection (DAPT), perpetuities rules, decanting flexibility, privacy.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
For long-duration trusts, situs choice meaningfully affects net-of-tax wealth across decades.

## VARIATIONS
- Delaware: trust law tradition, decanting, dynasty
- South Dakota: no state income tax on trust; strong DAPT; long perpetuities
- Nevada: strong DAPT, no state income tax, flexible decanting
- Wyoming: no state income tax, strong privacy
- Alaska: original DAPT state

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Worked example
$10M dynasty trust earning 7%; 50-year horizon:
- Georgia state tax on trust income: 5.19% × $700K = $36K/year
- South Dakota: $0
- 50-year compound difference: ~$25M-$40M of additional family wealth

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Choose situs based on goals
2. Engage institutional trustee in chosen state
3. Trust document with appropriate situs clauses
4. Administration discipline maintained (trustee acts in chosen state)
5. Avoid taxing-state nexus
