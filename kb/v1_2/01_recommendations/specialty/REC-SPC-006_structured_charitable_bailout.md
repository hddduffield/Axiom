## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Material appreciated asset
  - Charitable intent
  - Desire to preserve wealth for heirs (CRT alone disinherits)
  - Insurability for life insurance

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Pure charitable intent
  - Cannot qualify for insurance
```

### Natural-language explanation
Combines CRUT (sale of appreciated asset, charitable deduction, lifetime income) with wealth-replacement ILIT (life insurance owned by ILIT, funded with CRUT income or other dollars). Donor gets income; charity gets remainder; family gets ILIT death benefit replacing what they would have inherited.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Insurance not feasible
- Charitable scale insufficient

## WHAT IT IS
Combined structure:
1. Donor contributes appreciated asset to CRUT (REC-CHR-003); deduction; lifetime income
2. CRUT income (or separate funding) makes annual exclusion gifts to ILIT
3. ILIT purchases life insurance on donor (or both spouses)
4. At donor's death: ILIT receives death benefit (income-tax-free); CRUT remainder to charity
5. Family receives ILIT death benefit; charity receives CRUT remainder

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
For HNW with both charitable and family-preservation intent.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Worked example
$5M appreciated asset:
- CRUT: 5% unitrust → ~$250K/year income; ~$1M deduction; charitable remainder ~$5M+
- ILIT: $5M survivorship UL on couple; premium $50K-$80K/year (Crummey gifts)
- At deaths: family $5M+ from ILIT; charity $5M+ from CRUT
- Without structure: family ~$2-3M after estate tax; no charity
