# [REC-SPC-008] — Section 1031 Like-Kind Exchange (Real Estate)

## METADATA
- **ID:** REC-SPC-008
- **Status:** Active
- **Category:** Specialty
- **Engagement archetypes:** Investment real estate
- **Plan section placement:** "Real Estate / Tax"
- **Last verified:** April 2026

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Investment real estate sale contemplated
  - Material gain
  - Replacement property identifiable

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Personal residence (1031 doesn't apply)
  - Cash-out the goal
  - No replacement available within timeline
```

### Natural-language explanation
§1031 allows tax-deferred exchange of investment real estate for like-kind investment real estate. Strict timing: 45 days to identify, 180 days to close. Properly executed, defers gain; potentially eliminates at death via basis step-up.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Personal-use property
- Timeline cannot be met
- No qualified intermediary

## WHAT IT IS
Tax-deferred exchange under IRC §1031:
- Sell relinquished property
- Identify replacement within 45 days
- Close within 180 days
- Use qualified intermediary (no constructive receipt)
- Replacement value equal/greater (or recognize "boot")

OBBBA: §1031 limited to real property post-TCJA; OBBBA didn't expand.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Defers significant tax for investment real estate. Combined with basis step-up at death, can permanently eliminate gain.

## VARIATIONS
- Forward exchange (sell first, then buy)
- Reverse exchange (buy first, then sell — uses parking entity)
- Improvement exchange
- DST replacement (REC-SPC-009)

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK
- Capital gain deferred × applicable rate
- Depreciation recapture deferred
- Compounding across multiple exchanges
- Eventual basis step-up at death

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Engage qualified intermediary (QI) BEFORE relinquished closing
2. Identify replacement within 45 days
3. Close within 180 days
4. Documentation
5. Form 8824 with tax return

## SEQUENCING DEPENDENCIES
- COORDINATED WITH: REC-SPC-009 (DST replacement)

## DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST
- [ ] QI engaged before closing

- [ ] Form 8824

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Constructive receipt (no QI; or owner receives proceeds)
- Missing 45-day deadline
- Boot received → recognized
- Replacement not "like-kind" (rare with real estate post-TCJA)

## COORDINATION NOTES
- PSA: strategy
- CPA: Form 8824; basis tracking
- Attorney: transaction documents
- QI: mandatory specialty
- Real estate broker: primary execution

## CLIENT CONVERSATION FRAMING
> "If we sell {property} as a straight transaction, you face $${gain} of capital gain plus depreciation recapture. Through §1031, we defer all of that into a replacement. Strict timeline: 45 days identify, 180 days close. QI engaged before relinquished sale closes — non-negotiable."

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- Strict timing
- QI mandatory
- Real property only (post-TCJA)
- Boot creates partial recognition
