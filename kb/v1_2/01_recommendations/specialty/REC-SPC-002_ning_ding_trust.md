# [REC-SPC-002] — NING / DING Trust

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Material capital gains expected
  - Home state income tax > 6% (Georgia at 5.19% borderline)
  - Charitable intent or family transfer aligned

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Anti-NING legislation (CA, NY hostile)
  - Georgia residency unless very large gain
```

### Natural-language explanation
A NING (Nevada) or DING (Delaware) Incomplete-gift Non-Grantor Trust is structured to (a) avoid grantor-trust treatment for income tax and (b) sited in no-tax state. Result: trust income avoids high home-state tax. Used most often for capital gains in connection with sale events.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Anti-NING state
- Insufficient gain to justify

## WHAT IT IS
Irrevocable non-grantor trust:
- Sited in NV/DE/SD/WY/AK
- Distribution committee (not grantor) — gift incomplete for gift tax
- Non-grantor for income tax — trust pays own tax federally
- State tax: $0 in chosen situs

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT (rare in PSA's lane)
For California (13.3%), New York (10.9%+), New Jersey (10.75%) clients with material gains, NING saves 10%+ of gain. For Georgia at 5.19%, calculus rarely justifies.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Worked example (CA client)
$10M capital gain expected:
- California tax: 13.3% × $10M = $1.33M
- NING shifts gain; state tax = $0
- Admin cost: $25K-$50K
- Net savings: ~$1.27M

For Georgia: $519K savings at 5.19%; admin cost similar; tighter calculus.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

4. Asset transfer pre-sale
