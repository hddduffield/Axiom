# [REC-SPC-010] — Pre-Sale Restructuring for §1202 Multiplication

## METADATA

- **Status:** Advanced
- **Category:** Specialty

- **Plan section placement:** "Pre-Transaction" → "Tax Optimization"

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Client holds qualified §1202 QSBS
  - Holding period satisfied or in progress
  - Material gain expected exceeding per-issuer cap
  - Family members or trusts available for gifting
```

### Natural-language explanation
§1202 exclusion is per-taxpayer, per-issuer. Gifting QSBS to family members or non-grantor trusts pre-sale "multiplies" the available exclusion — each new holder has their own $15M cap (post-OBBBA). For very large gains, can preserve $30M-$80M+ of additional exclusion.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Stock not qualifying §1202
- Anti-step-transaction concerns (must not occur in close proximity to sale)

## WHAT IT IS
Pre-sale gifts of QSBS to:
- Spouse (limited benefit)
- Children (uses lifetime exemption)
- Non-grantor trusts (each is separate taxpayer with its own §1202 cap)
- Multi-state non-grantor trusts (NING-style for state tax also)

Each recipient has independent $15M cap. Holding period tacks from giver.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
For founders with very large §1202 gains, multiplication preserves significant exclusion capacity beyond single-cap structure.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Worked example
Founder expects $50M gain on QSBS:
- Without multiplication: $15M excluded; $35M taxed at 23.8% = $8.3M tax
- With multiplication (founder + spouse + 3 trusts = 5 caps × $15M = $75M capacity): full $50M excluded
- Savings: $8.3M

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Confirm §1202 qualification
2. Estate planning analysis (recipients)
3. Gifts pre-sale (well before binding LOI; document gift purpose independent of sale)
4. Holding-period tacking
5. Sale; each recipient claims §1202 exclusion
6. Coordinated tax filing

- Trust structure as grantor trust (caps don't multiply)
- Inadequate documentation
- Holding period gap
