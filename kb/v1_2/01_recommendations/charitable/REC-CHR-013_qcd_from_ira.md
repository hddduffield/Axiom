# [REC-CHR-013] — Qualified Charitable Distribution (QCD) from IRA

## METADATA
- **ID:** REC-CHR-013
- **Status:** Active
- **Category:** Charitable
- **Engagement archetypes:** RMD-age clients
- **Plan section placement:** "Recommendations — Charitable / Tax Planning"

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Donor age 70½+ (QCD eligibility)
  - Traditional IRA balance
  - Charitable intent
  - At RMD age (current 73 or 75 by birth year per SECURE 2.0)

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Below age 70½
  - No IRA balance
  - DAF more efficient (QCDs cannot go to DAF)
```

### Natural-language explanation
QCDs allow IRA holders age 70½+ to direct up to $108,000/year (2026 indexed) directly from IRA to qualified charity (not DAF, not foundation, not supporting org). The distribution is excluded from taxable income — better than taking the RMD and donating the cash, since the latter only generates a deduction limited by AGI rules. QCDs satisfy RMDs.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Age below 70½
- No IRA
- Charity is DAF or private foundation (ineligible recipients)

## WHAT IT IS
Direct trustee-to-trustee transfer (or check from IRA payable to charity) up to $108,000/year (2026; indexed annually). Excluded from gross income. Can satisfy RMD requirement. Recipient must be qualified public charity (NOT DAF, foundation, or supporting org).

SECURE 2.0 also added: one-time $54,000 (2026 indexed) transfer to a CGA, CRT, or CRUT — known as "Legacy IRA" provision.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
For HNW clients in retirement: QCD beats taking RMD + donating cash because:
- Excludes from AGI (vs. RMD increases AGI; deduction may be limited)
- Doesn't increase Medicare IRMAA (RMD does)
- Doesn't reduce Social Security taxability calculations
- Counts toward RMD requirement

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Income tax avoided on RMD-equivalent
- Medicare IRMAA savings
- Social Security taxation savings

### Worked example
75-year-old client with $50K RMD, $50K of charitable intent:
- Without QCD: RMD adds $50K to AGI; charitable deduction $50K subject to 60% AGI limit; net tax ~$0 but AGI raised → IRMAA + SS taxation impact
- With QCD: $50K direct to charity; AGI not affected; RMD satisfied; no IRMAA cliff; SS taxation calculation unchanged
- IRMAA savings: $1K-$3K depending on cliff

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Confirm client age 70½+
2. Identify qualified charity (NOT DAF/foundation)
3. Direct IRA custodian to make QCD payment
4. Custodian issues 1099-R; tax filing reflects exclusion (Schedule 1 / Form 1040 marking)
5. Annual repetition

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Sending to DAF (ineligible)
- Receiving check personally then forwarding (loses QCD treatment — must be direct)
- Forgetting to mark on tax return (audit triggers)
- Exceeding annual limit ($108K in 2026; indexed)
