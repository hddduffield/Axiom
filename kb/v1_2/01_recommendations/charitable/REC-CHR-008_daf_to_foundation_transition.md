# [REC-CHR-008] — DAF → Private Foundation Transition

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - DAF in place
  - Scale and engagement now justify foundation (typically $25M+)
  - Family ready for foundation governance
```

### Natural-language explanation
DAF can be the on-ramp to private foundation. Once scale and engagement justify the additional administration, transition the DAF balance to a newly-formed foundation. Mechanics straightforward: foundation receives DAF grant equal to balance; DAF closed (or maintained at smaller scale for specific purposes).

### Hard disqualifiers
- Foundation premature
- DAF and foundation both useful (run in parallel rather than transitioning)

## WHAT IT IS
Coordinated transition:
1. Form private foundation
2. Recommend DAF grant of full balance to new foundation (DAF distributions to private foundations are standard; check sponsor for specific rules)
3. Foundation receives funds; close DAF or maintain at minimum balance

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Many HNW philanthropy paths move from DAF (initial flexibility) to foundation (control and ongoing engagement). Transition is mechanically simple; governance shift is the real change.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Form foundation (REC-CHR-007)
2. DAF sponsor confirms it can grant to private foundation
3. Recommend grant from DAF to foundation
4. Foundation receives, deploys per plan
5. Close DAF or reduce

## SEQUENCING DEPENDENCIES
- **PREREQUISITE:** REC-CHR-007 (foundation established)
