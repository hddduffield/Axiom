## ⚠️ LANDMINE WARNING

**This recommendation is OFF by default. Conservation easements are an IRS enforcement priority; "syndicated conservation easements" in particular have been on the Dirty Dozen for years and are subject to substantial penalty regimes.**

The IRS has won the overwhelming majority of conservation easement cases at the Tax Court. The OBBBA increased certain conservation easement deduction limits but did NOT reverse the IRS's enforcement posture on syndicated easements.

**The firm's house position is: do NOT recommend or implement conservation easements unless (a) the client owns the land directly with genuine conservation value, (b) the appraisal is conservative and defensible, (c) qualified counsel and appraisers are engaged, and (d) the client accepts likely audit. Syndicated easements are categorically unacceptable.**

### Natural-language explanation
A conservation easement is a permanent restriction on the use of real property, granted to a qualified conservation organization (land trust). The donor receives a charitable deduction for the difference between the property's value before and after the easement. The structure is legitimate and important for genuine conservation. It has been heavily abused — particularly through "syndicated" structures where investors buy into partnerships that purchase land, donate easements at inflated valuations, and take outsize deductions.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Syndicated structure
- Inflated appraisal
- Recently acquired property
- Promoter-driven engagement

## WHAT IT IS
Permanent restriction on real property granted to qualified conservation organization. Donor:
- Permanently gives up specified development / use rights
- Land trust enforces restriction in perpetuity
- Donor takes charitable deduction = (FMV before easement) - (FMV after easement)

## WHY THIS IS LANDMINE
- IRS Dirty Dozen for syndicated conservation easements since 2017
- TCJA codified additional disclosure requirements
- 2022 SECURE 2.0 Act §605 capped deductions for partnership-held syndicated easements at 2.5× basis
- Tax Court rulings consistently against aggressive valuations
- §170(h) requirements heavily scrutinized

## WHEN APPROPRIATE (rare)
- Family-owned land with documented ecological value
- Owner held for years (not opportunistic acquisition)
- Conservative appraisal (defensible 2x or less appreciation claim)
- Local land trust with track record
- Client comfortable with audit likelihood
