## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Charitable intent + income desire
  - Smaller funding amount than CRT (typically <$1M)
  - Charity offers a pooled income fund

DISQUALIFY if:
  - CRT scale appropriate (use REC-CHR-003 instead)
  - Charity doesn't offer PIF
```

### Natural-language explanation
A pooled income fund (PIF) is operated by a charity; donors contribute and receive pro-rata share of the pool's income for life. Smaller-scale alternative to CRT for donors without enough scale to justify CRT setup.

### Hard disqualifiers
- CRT scale better
- No charity-operated PIF available

## WHAT IT IS
Charity-operated pooled investment fund. Donor contributes; receives units in pool; receives proportionate share of pool's income annually. At death, principal goes to charity. Less customizable than CRT but no separate trust setup needed.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT (rarely)
Smaller-scale charitable + income use cases. CRT is preferred for HNW PSA-style clients.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Identify charity offering PIF
2. Contribution
3. Annual income distributions
4. Tax reporting
