## METADATA

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Qualified plan in place
  - Plan document has not been restated within Cycle 4 (volume submitter / individually designed plan timing)
  - OR statutory changes have occurred requiring document update (SECURE Act, SECURE 2.0)

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Restatement complete within current cycle
```

### Natural-language explanation
IRS requires periodic restatement of qualified plan documents to incorporate statutory changes. Pre-approved volume submitter plans operate on a 6-year cycle; individually designed plans on different timing. Failure to restate timely jeopardizes plan qualification — a major risk that can disqualify decades of contributions.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Recently restated; current

## WHAT IT IS
A complete redrafting of the plan document to incorporate all interim statutory and regulatory changes. Volume submitter plans (most small business plans) restate on a 6-year cycle; individually designed plans on EGTRRA/PPA cycles. SECURE 2.0 changes drive a major restatement need for plans not yet updated.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Failure to timely restate puts plan qualification at risk — IRS can disqualify the plan, taxing all participants on accumulated balances. Restatement is non-negotiable compliance.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Identify plan's restatement cycle (TPA/recordkeeper tracks)
2. Engage TPA or specialist counsel for restatement
3. Adopt restated document via board resolution
4. SPD updated and distributed to participants
