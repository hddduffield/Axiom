# [REC-RET-006] — SEP-IRA / Solo 401(k) for Spousal or Consulting Income

## METADATA
- **ID:** REC-RET-006
- **Status:** Active
- **Category:** Retirement
- **Engagement archetypes:** Post-Exit (consulting income), All (spouse with self-employment income)
- **Plan section placement:** "Recommendations — Retirement & Benefits"
- **Last verified:** April 2026

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Self-employment income (1099, consulting, freelance) for client or spouse
  - No employees (other than spouse) — Solo 401(k) requires no common-law employees beyond spouse
  - Income substantial enough to make plan worthwhile ($50K+/year)

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Has employees other than spouse (Solo 401(k) becomes regular 401(k); SEP includes employees)
  - Already has primary 401(k) at deferral cap (deferral overlap; profit-sharing layer still possible)
```

### Natural-language explanation
Self-employment / consulting income — common post-exit when client transitions to consulting role, or for spouse with side income — supports SEP-IRA or Solo 401(k). Solo 401(k) generally superior (allows deferrals + profit sharing; SEP only profit-sharing). Maximum contribution: roughly 25% of net earnings up to $72,000 cap (2026).

### Hard disqualifiers
- Common-law employees beyond spouse → Solo 401(k) doesn't apply
- No earned income

## WHAT IT IS
**Solo 401(k):** Owner-only 401(k) plan; allows employee deferrals ($24,500 + $8,000 catch-up) PLUS employer profit sharing (~25% of compensation) up to combined §415(c) cap of $72,000 ($80,000 with catch-up).

**SEP-IRA:** Simpler; profit-sharing-only. ~25% of compensation up to $72,000 (2026).

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Captures retirement deductions on consulting/spousal SE income that would otherwise be unsheltered. For Marcus-Catherine post-exit example: Catherine's consulting income through Solo 401(k) shelters significant additional retirement contribution.

## VARIATIONS
- **Solo 401(k):** allows deferral + profit sharing; can do Roth deferrals; loans available
- **SEP-IRA:** profit-sharing only; simpler; no Roth option (until SECURE 2.0 changes)
- **Solo Roth 401(k):** Roth deferrals + traditional profit sharing
- **SIMPLE IRA:** smaller limits, simpler still; rare in PSA's lane

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Deferral capacity (Solo 401(k) only)
- Profit-sharing capacity
- Tax savings at marginal rate

### Worked example
Spouse with $200K of self-employment income, age 50:
- Solo 401(k) deferral: $24,500 + $8,000 catch-up = $32,500
- Profit-sharing layer (~25% of net earnings after SE tax): ~$37,000
- Combined: $69,500 (under $80,000 §415(c) + catch-up cap)
- Tax savings at 32-37% bracket: ~$22K-$26K/year

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Confirm no employees beyond spouse
2. Establish plan: Solo 401(k) (preferred) or SEP-IRA
3. Plan document — Fidelity, Schwab, or specialty TPA can establish at low cost for Solo 401(k)
4. Defer from each pay period (if W-2'ed) or by year-end (if 1099)
5. Profit-sharing contribution by tax-filing deadline incl. extensions
6. Annual Form 5500 if Solo 401(k) over $250K balance

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Hiring employees later → Solo 401(k) becomes ineligible (or requires employee inclusion)
- Misunderstanding compensation calculation (net SE income after SE tax for SEP)
- Failing to fund by deadline
- Multiple plans at deferral cap (deferrals are per-individual, not per-plan)

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS

- Consulting income through corporate entity needs different treatment
- Prior-year earned income limits the contribution
