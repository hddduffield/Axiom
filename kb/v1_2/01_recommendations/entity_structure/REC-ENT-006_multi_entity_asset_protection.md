### Natural-language explanation
Architect multiple entities to limit cross-entity liability exposure. Operating entity, property entity, IP entity, family management entity — each with own liability boundary.

---

## WHAT IT IS

Multi-entity structure with liability boundaries between operations and assets:
- **Operating entity** — runs the business, faces customer/employee/vendor liability
- **Property entity** — owns real estate, leases to operations (REC-ENT-001)
- **IP entity** — owns trademarks, patents, software (licenses to operations)
- **Family management entity** — pays family members for legitimate services to operations
- **Holdco** — owns operating, property, IP entities

Each entity has its own LLC veil. Liability of one doesn't reach the others.

---

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

Asset protection layered with structural / tax planning. The same structures that enable estate planning (Holdco), tax planning (property separation, family management), and §1202 (C-Corp) also provide liability isolation.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

1. Map current entity structure
2. Identify gaps and overlaps
3. Coordinate with REC-ENT-001 (real estate), REC-ENT-002 (F-reorg), and ongoing operations
4. Each entity must observe corporate formalities (separate accounts, separate records, real economic dealings)
5. Attorney drafts operating agreements for each
6. Annual maintenance — corporate formalities, separate insurance, separate documentation

## COMMON MISTAKES & AUDIT TRIGGERS
- **Veil-piercing risk** — commingling, undercapitalization, inadequate formalities
- **Inter-entity transactions at non-FMV** — disregarded transactions; veil weakened
- **Same management and same accounts** — alter-ego attack
