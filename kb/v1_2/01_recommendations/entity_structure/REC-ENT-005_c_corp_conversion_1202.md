### Natural-language explanation
Convert (or originate) Holdco as C-Corporation. New shares issued post-conversion qualify as QSBS under §1202. Held 5+ years, future appreciation up to $15M / 10× basis is excluded from federal capital gains tax.

### Hard disqualifiers
- SSTB business
- Gross assets > $75M at conversion
- Transaction window < 3 years

---

## WHAT IT IS

Strategic conversion of Holdco S-Corp to C-Corp via S-election revocation, OR formation of Holdco as C-Corp from inception during F-reorg. Stock issued at C-Corp formation/conversion is QSBS subject to §1202 rules.

Under OBBBA: 3 yr / 50%, 4 yr / 75%, 5 yr / 100% exclusion; $15M cap or 10× basis; $75M gross asset cap at issuance.

---

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

For pre-exit clients with long window AND substantial expected appreciation, §1202 exclusion can save $3-15M+ per qualifying shareholder. Multiplication via spouse and trusts can scale.

PSA's house position: highly fact-specific. C-Corp drag (~21% federal entity-level on operating profits, double-tax on dividends) often offsets §1202 benefit unless transaction is large and appreciation post-conversion is substantial.

3. C-Corp formation or conversion
4. Stock issued at conversion documented as QSBS-eligible
5. Annual maintenance of QSBS qualifications: active business test, gross asset test, no taint events
6. Coordinate with REC-TAX-008 multiplication strategy via gifting
