
### Natural-language explanation
Private market exposure (private equity, private credit, real estate, venture, hedge funds) can add diversification and return potential beyond traditional public markets. Targeted allocation typically 10-25% of investable assets for HNW; access via fund-of-funds or direct LP commitments. Illiquidity, vintage diversification, and manager selection are central.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Liquidity needs preclude
- Insufficient diversification of base portfolio
- Lack of qualified purchaser status

## WHAT IT IS
Allocation to private market vehicles:
- **Private equity** (buyout, growth) — fund-of-funds or direct LP
- **Private credit** (direct lending, mezzanine, distressed)
- **Real estate** (private REITs, opportunistic, value-add)
- **Venture capital** (early/late stage, sector-specific)
- **Hedge funds** (long-short, multi-strat, distressed, event-driven)
- **Infrastructure**

Vintage diversification: stagger commitments across years to avoid single-vintage concentration.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
For HNW post-liquidity, traditional public market allocation may underweight diversification opportunities. Private markets can add return premium (illiquidity premium of 200-400bps historically) and reduce correlation. Requires sophistication.

## VARIATIONS
- **Fund-of-funds:** broader diversification, additional fee layer
- **Direct LP commitments:** lower cost, requires more diligence
- **Interval funds / closed-end:** semi-liquid private exposure with redemption windows
- **Direct co-invest:** alongside professional GP; very HNW; requires deal-by-deal evaluation

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Expected return premium vs. public benchmark (200-400bps historical)
- Diversification benefit (correlations to public markets)
- Fee drag (high — 2/20 typical for direct PE; less for fund-of-funds; even more for hedge funds)
- J-curve (negative early returns; positive later)

### Worked example
$5M private allocation across vintages and strategies:
- Expected long-term net return: 10-12% (vs. 7-8% public equity)
- 10-year cumulative outperformance: $3M-$5M depending on returns
- Fee drag: 2-3%/year of assets
- Liquidity: 7-10 years for full return of capital + carry

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Define target allocation (% of investable assets, ranges by sub-strategy)
2. Vintage planning — pace commitments over 3-5 years
3. Manager / fund selection — diligence, IPS conformity, fee analysis
4. Subscription documents, capital call planning
5. Ongoing reporting and re-up decisions
6. Annual portfolio review

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Single-vintage concentration
- Manager selection error (manager dispersion in private is huge)
- Underestimating capital calls vs. distribution timing
- Over-allocation creating liquidity squeeze
- Failing to plan for future commitments (don't commit so much you can't add later)

## COORDINATION NOTES

- **CPA:** K-1 reporting from each fund (significant complexity)
- **Attorney:** subscription agreement review for material commitments

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- Illiquidity is real — not just paper
- Manager dispersion in private markets is much wider than public
- Fee structures are heavy
- K-1 reporting complexity adds tax-prep cost
- IPS discipline mandatory to avoid concentration
