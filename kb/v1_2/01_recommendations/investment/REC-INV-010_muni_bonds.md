## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Owner in 32%+ federal bracket

DISQUALIFY if:

  - Below 24% bracket (taxable corporate bonds may yield more after tax)
```

### Natural-language explanation
Municipal bond interest is generally tax-free at federal level (IRC §103). For high-bracket clients, after-tax yield often exceeds taxable bond yield of similar credit quality. In-state munis additionally exempt from state tax. Standard allocation for HNW taxable fixed income.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Below relevant tax bracket threshold

## WHAT IT IS
Municipal bonds issued by state and local governments, used to fund public projects. Interest is federally tax-exempt; in-state issues are also state-tax-exempt for residents. Various structures: GO bonds, revenue bonds, ETFs (national or state-specific), individual bond ladders.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
For high-bracket investor: 4% taxable yield at 37% = 2.52% after-tax; 3% muni yield = 3% after-tax. Muni wins meaningfully. Real-time yield comparisons drive specific allocation.

## VARIATIONS
- National muni ETF/SMA (broad diversification)
- State-specific muni (Georgia muni for GA residents — additional state tax exemption)
- Individual bond ladder (held to maturity)
- High-yield muni (riskier; higher yield)

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Tax-exempt yield × adjusted comparison to taxable equivalents
- State tax exemption for in-state issues

### Worked example
HNW client at 37% federal + 5.19% Georgia, $2M fixed income allocation:
- Taxable corporate bonds at 5%: $100K interest, $42K tax = $58K after-tax
- Muni at 3.5%: $70K interest, $0 tax = $70K after-tax
- Annual benefit: $12K of additional after-tax income on $2M

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Determine fixed-income allocation amount
2. Choose vehicle (ETF, SMA, bond ladder)
3. State preference: GA-specific for additional state tax savings
4. Credit-quality discipline: minimum AA-/A1 typical
5. Annual review of yield environment and credit conditions
