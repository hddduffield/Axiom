### Natural-language explanation
Personal cash, like business cash, benefits from layering: monthly operating (checking), 6-12 months emergency (HYSA / MMF), 12-36 month known expenses (Treasury ladder), beyond that → invested in portfolio. Without layering, HNW clients often hold $500K-$2M in checking earning 0.1% — meaningful foregone yield.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Already structured

## WHAT IT IS
Four-layer personal cash structure:
1. **Operating:** 1-2 months expenses; checking; minimal yield
2. **Emergency:** 6-12 months expenses; high-yield savings or money market; ~4.5% currently
3. **Known short-term:** 12-36 months for college, real estate down payment, etc.; Treasury ladder or short bond portfolio; ~4.5-4.8%
4. **Long-term:** beyond 36 months horizon → invested per asset allocation, not "cash"

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
$1M in checking at 0.1% earns $1,000/year. Same $1M layered: $200K checking ($200), $400K HYSA at 4.5% ($18,000), $400K Treasury ladder at 4.7% ($18,800). Total $37,000 vs $1,000 — $36,000/year improvement on cash that wasn't invested but is now properly placed.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Yield differential
- Liquidity matching to actual needs
- Treasury direct (no credit risk on sovereign holdings)

### Worked example (HNW family)
Family with $1.5M in checking + $200K in low-yield savings:
- Current yield: $700/year
- Restructured: $200K operating ($200), $700K HYSA at 4.5% ($31,500), $400K 1-3 year Treasury ($18,800), $400K to invested portfolio
- New cash yield: $50,500
- Plus $400K shifted to portfolio earning expected 7% = $28,000
- Combined improvement: ~$77,800/year of yield/return

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Identify total cash and current placement
2. Determine appropriate layer sizes (operating: 1-2 mo; emergency: 6-12 mo; near-term: 12-36 mo)
3. Open vehicles: HYSA (Marcus, Ally, etc.), brokerage MMF, Treasury Direct
4. Move funds across layers
5. Quarterly monitoring
