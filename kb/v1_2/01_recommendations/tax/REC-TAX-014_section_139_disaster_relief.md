## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if:
  - Federally-declared disaster affects business location or employees
  - Employer wants to provide tax-advantaged relief to affected employees
```

### Natural-language explanation
Under IRC §139, employer can make tax-free payments to employees affected by federally-declared disasters. Payments are deductible to employer; tax-free to employee (not W-2, no FICA).

---

## WHAT IT IS

Qualified disaster relief payments under IRC §139 — payments to employees for personal expenses arising from a federally-declared disaster (hurricane, flood, wildfire, pandemic). Recipient pays no federal income tax on the payment; employer deducts as ordinary business expense.

---

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

Highly tax-efficient way to support affected employees. Eligible expenses include personal/family medical, dental, transportation, repair of personal residence, food/clothing replacement.

---

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

Per-employee benefit: payment × (employee's marginal rate + FICA) saved
- Example: $5K payment to employee at 32% combined rate saves ~$1,600 in employee tax + $383 FICA = ~$2K of effective benefit per $5K, vs. equivalent W-2 bonus

---

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

1. Confirm federally-declared disaster status (FEMA designation).
2. Document program: eligibility, payment levels, expense categories covered.
3. Distribute payments via separate check (not payroll), with documentation memo.
4. Employer takes deduction; no W-2 / 1099 reporting required for §139 payments.

## COMMON MISTAKES & AUDIT TRIGGERS
- Payments not tied to a federally-declared disaster
- Lump-sum payments without expense rationale
- Treating as bonus on W-2 (defeats §139)

---

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- Requires federally-declared disaster
- Cannot be substitute for regular wages or for personal benefit of business owners
- Owners themselves can receive §139 payments only if they are also employees affected as employees, not as owners
