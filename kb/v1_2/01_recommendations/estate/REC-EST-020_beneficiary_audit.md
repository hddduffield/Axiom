## WHAT IT IS

Comprehensive review and update of all beneficiary designations on assets that pass outside probate:
- Retirement accounts (401(k), IRA, Roth, 403(b), etc.)
- Life insurance (personal-owned and ILIT-owned, with confirmation that ILIT-owned doesn't have direct personal beneficiaries)
- Annuities
- Transfer-on-Death (TOD) accounts (brokerage)
- Payable-on-Death (POD) accounts (banking)
- HSA / FSA accounts

---

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

Beneficiary designations are the SINGLE most-overlooked failure in estate planning. They override will and trust provisions. Common failures:
- Ex-spouse still listed (post-divorce)
- Deceased beneficiary still listed
- No contingent beneficiary (asset goes to estate, then through probate)
- "Estate" listed as primary (forces probate of asset that should have passed directly)
- Outdated allocations not reflecting current children/heirs

The cost of getting this wrong: probate, family disputes, unintended beneficiaries, loss of stretch benefits on inherited retirement assets.

---

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK
- Probate avoidance: 1-3% of asset value (legal fees)
- Family-dispute avoidance: highly variable, often catastrophic
- Stretch IRA benefit preservation: SECURE Act limits stretch to 10 years for non-spouse beneficiaries; designations matter for eligible designated beneficiaries

---

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

1. Inventory all accounts with beneficiary designations
2. Pull current designations from each (statements, custodian forms)
3. Compare against current estate plan and family situation
4. Update via custodian forms (each custodian has its own form)
5. Confirm receipt and recording
6. Document all designations in central file
7. Annual review

## COMMON MISTAKES & AUDIT TRIGGERS
- **Updates not submitted** to custodian (drafted but not filed)
- **Confirmations not retained** — at death, can't prove update was made
- **Trust as beneficiary without proper "see-through" language** — defeats stretch IRA benefit
- **Old beneficiary forms supersede new** — some custodians require specific form versions
