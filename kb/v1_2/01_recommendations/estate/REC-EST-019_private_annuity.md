## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Donor older with declining health considerations
  - Asset to transfer would otherwise face estate tax
  - Donor willing to give up principal in exchange for life annuity
```

### Natural-language explanation
Sale of asset to family member in exchange for unsecured promise to pay annual annuity for donor's life. At donor's death, annuity ceases; asset belongs to buyer free of estate tax. Tax treatment changed under 2006 regulations — full gain recognized at sale (rather than spread over annuity period).

### Hard disqualifiers
- Annuity rate inadequate (gift element)
- Donor in robust health (defeats purpose of life-contingent annuity)

---

## WHAT IT IS

Donor sells asset to family member (typically trust) in exchange for unsecured promise to pay annual annuity for donor's life. §7520 mortality tables value the annuity. At donor's death: annuity terminates; buyer keeps the asset.

**Critical 2006 regulation change:** §72(b)(2) and Prop. Reg. §1.1001-1(j) require donor to recognize entire gain at sale of asset (no longer spread). This dramatically reduces appeal vs. pre-2006 era.

---

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

Limited applicability post-2006 regulation change. Some practitioners still use for very specific situations: terminally ill donor (where annuity will be very short), assets with substantial built-in losses, or scenarios where front-loaded gain recognition is acceptable.

PSA's house position: rarely used. Specialist tool only.

---

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

Highly fact-specific. Net analysis must compare:
- Front-loaded gain recognition (cost)
- Annuity value at §7520 / mortality tables
- Estate exclusion at donor's death (benefit)

---

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

1. Specialist counsel analyzes fit (rare).
2. Actuarial calculation of annuity using §7520 + mortality.
3. Sale documents and unsecured annuity promise.
4. Donor recognizes gain at sale (post-2006 rules).
5. Annual annuity payments.
6. At donor's death, annuity terminates.
