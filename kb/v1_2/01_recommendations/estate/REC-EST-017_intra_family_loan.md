# [REC-EST-017] — Intra-Family Loan at AFR

## METADATA

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ANY of:
  - Family member needs liquidity (home purchase, business start)
  - Donor wants to capture spread between AFR and family member's use of capital
  - Goal includes wealth transfer without using exemption
```

### Natural-language explanation
Donor lends to family member at AFR; borrower pays interest. If family member's investment return exceeds AFR, the spread accrues to family member outside donor's estate.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Loan documented as gift (no real expectation of repayment)
- Below-AFR rate (creates gift element under §7872)

---

## WHAT IT IS

Bona fide loan from one family member to another (or family trust to individual) at the AFR for the loan term. Documented promissory note; payments made on schedule; collateral if appropriate. Borrower invests proceeds; spread between investment return and AFR accrues outside lender's estate.

---

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

Simple, low-friction wealth-transfer technique. AFR is currently 4-5% (much lower than expected investment returns). Lender doesn't use lifetime exemption (it's a loan, not a gift). Spread benefits borrower (who is presumably a child or family trust).

Particularly useful for: home purchases by adult children (where market mortgage rate is higher than AFR), seed capital for child's business, refinancing existing arms-length debt at lower AFR.

---

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Worked example
- Loan amount: $1M
- Term: 9 years (long-term AFR territory)
- AFR: 4.5%
- Family member invests at 7% return
- Spread captured by family member: 7% - 4.5% = 2.5% × $1M = $25K/year × 9 years = $225K
- $225K of wealth transferred without using exemption

---

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

1. CPA confirms current AFR for note term
2. Promissory note drafted with payment schedule, AFR rate, collateral (if any)
3. Loan funded
4. Annual interest payments per schedule (cash) or accrued
5. Lender reports interest income; borrower may deduct interest if loan qualifies
6. At maturity, principal repaid

## COMMON MISTAKES & AUDIT TRIGGERS
- **Below-AFR rate** — gift element on shortfall (§7872)
- **No real expectation of repayment** — IRS recharacterizes as gift
- **Failure to make payments** — IRS attacks economic substance
- **Forgiveness of debt later** — gift at forgiveness; uses exemption
