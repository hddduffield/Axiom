## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - First spouse death
  - Decedent's exemption not fully used
  - Surviving spouse may benefit from DSUE
  - Filing deadline window: 9 months + 6 month auto extension; OR Rev. Proc. 2022-32 5-year late relief
```

### Natural-language explanation
Election made on first spouse's Form 706 to transfer Deceased Spouse's Unused Exemption (DSUE) to surviving spouse. Surviving spouse can use DSUE on her own gifts during life or at death. Even small estates should consider this, since future appreciation may push surviving estate above own exemption.

---

## WHAT IT IS

Election under IRC §2010(c)(4) made on the deceased spouse's federal estate tax return (Form 706). Adds the unused portion of decedent's basic exclusion to the surviving spouse's exclusion.

For 2026 with $15M individual exemption: if first-to-die used $5M, surviving spouse adds $10M DSUE to her own $15M = $25M total exclusion available for her future gifts/estate.

---

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

DSUE preservation is essentially free insurance — costs only the Form 706 filing. Downside if not elected: surviving spouse's estate uses only her own exemption; deceased's unused exemption is lost.

For couples below combined exemption: portability may not seem necessary, but appreciation can cause surviving estate to exceed exemption later.

---

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK
- Worst case (no election): up to $15M of unused exemption lost
- At 40% federal estate tax: up to $6M of estate tax that could have been avoided

---

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

1. **Form 706 filed for first-to-die** even if no estate tax owed (election cannot be made on simplified or non-filing return)
2. **Within 9 months + 6-month extension** of date of death; OR
3. **Late portability under Rev. Proc. 2022-32**: within 5 years of date of death (relief available if estate didn't otherwise have to file)
4. **Document DSUE amount** and preserve for surviving spouse's records
5. **Coordinate with §645 election** for trust administration

## COMMON MISTAKES & AUDIT TRIGGERS
- **Failure to file Form 706** when no estate tax owed (election unavailable without filing)
- **Missing the 9-month deadline** without using late-portability relief
- **DSUE does NOT preserve GST exemption** — separate strategy needed for GST
