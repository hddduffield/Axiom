### Natural-language explanation
Election under IRC §645 to treat decedent's revocable trust as part of decedent's estate for income tax purposes. Allows fiscal-year filing for the trust, charitable set-aside benefits, and other estate-tax administrative advantages.

---

## WHAT IT IS

Election filed by trustee and executor (joint election) to treat a "qualified revocable trust" as part of the decedent's estate for income tax purposes during the election period (up to 2 years after death + 6 months for late portability). Allows the trust to:
- File on calendar or fiscal year (estate uses fiscal year; revocable trust normally calendar)
- Use the estate's charitable set-aside deduction
- Avoid filing two separate income tax returns
- Take advantage of estate-only deductions

---

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

In post-death administration, this election simplifies tax filings, may reduce overall income tax during administration, and provides timing flexibility. Default recommendation for any revocable-trust decedent unless specific facts argue against.

---

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK
- Modest: typically $5K-$30K of income tax savings during administration
- Administrative simplification

---

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

1. CPA and attorney coordinate election analysis
2. File Form 8855 (Election to Treat Qualified Revocable Trust as Part of Estate) within 2-year window
3. Trust files combined with estate (Form 1041)
4. At end of election period, trust resumes as separate entity
