## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Business with key employees
  - Owner already has PSA relationship
  - Desire to extend wealth-management benefit to key team
```

### Natural-language explanation
Offering financial planning as an executive benefit provides retention value at modest cost. Key employees get access to retirement planning, tax optimization, and basic estate work; business deducts the fee; employees value the benefit at well above its cost.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Conflict-of-interest issues

## WHAT IT IS
A retainer arrangement where the business pays for selected employees to receive financial planning services through PSA. Tiered access (executive vs. key contributor) common. Cost: $5K-$20K/employee/year typical.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Retention; team-wide financial wellness; integrates with overall HR strategy. For business: deductible expense; minimal cost; meaningful perceived value.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Define eligible employee tier
2. Establish service scope (planning, tax review, retirement modeling)
3. Engagement letter (business-paid)
4. Communication to eligible employees
5. Annual review

> "We extend financial planning services to your top {N} executives — covered by the business as a benefit. Costs about ${cost} per employee per year, fully deductible. They value it at well above that; you keep them retained and aligned. PSA is already in your firm; this is a clean extension."

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
