## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Owner is operationally critical (key-person concentration)
  - Transaction window 12-24 months
  - Existing team lacks one or more critical functions

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Bench already complete
  - Owner committed to staying long-term post-close anyway
```

### Natural-language explanation
Buyers heavily discount businesses where the owner is the operational center. Pre-transaction, the firm helps identify gaps in the management team and supports hiring/promoting to fill them — typically 1-3 senior roles. Each promoted/hired role increases buyer comfort with the post-close transition.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Bench already complete

## WHAT IT IS
A 12-18 month plan to recruit, promote, or develop senior team members who can operate the business without daily owner involvement. Critical functions: COO/operations, CFO/finance, sales leader, key technical lead. Each fills a slot the owner currently occupies.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Owner-dependent businesses sell at substantial discounts (sometimes 20-40% below comparable). Each operational gap closed is direct transaction value created.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Org chart analysis identifying critical function gaps
2. Decisions: hire externally, promote internally, or fractional contract
3. Recruiting / development plans
4. Compensation packages (often coordinated with stay bonus and SERP/equity)
5. Performance milestones aligned with transaction timing

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Hiring too late (no time for new hires to season)
- Compensation that doesn't survive transaction (need vesting through close)
- Failing to delegate genuinely (owner remains operational center despite hires)

## COORDINATION NOTES

## CLIENT CONVERSATION FRAMING
> "Buyers pay for businesses, not for owners. The more decisions and operations run without you in the room, the higher the multiple. Over the next 12-18 months, we close gaps in {critical_functions}. Each role filled is direct transaction value."

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- Real delegation required, not nominal hires
- Compensation must survive transaction timing
