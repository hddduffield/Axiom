### Natural-language explanation
A §162 executive bonus is a simple arrangement: business pays a "bonus" — taxable to executive, deductible to business — which executive uses to fund a personally-owned life insurance policy or other benefit. No §409A complexity, no vesting, no rabbi trust. Executive owns the asset immediately. Loss of retention leverage is the trade-off.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Need for vesting / clawback (use Restricted §162 instead — REC-SUC-004)

## WHAT IT IS
Standard W-2 bonus to executive specifically earmarked to fund a personal life insurance policy or other benefit. Business deducts the bonus; executive reports as W-2 income; uses post-tax proceeds to fund policy. Often "double-bonus" structure: bonus also covers the executive's tax.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Simple. No §409A complications. Executive owns the policy/asset. Useful for executive who values immediate ownership over potential SERP benefit.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Bonus paid to executive (deductible to business)
- Tax cost to executive (W-2 income; ~37% combined)
- Asset funded (life insurance with cash value, etc.)
- Optional: "double bonus" covers tax

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Identify executive and target benefit
2. Determine bonus amount (with or without tax gross-up)
3. Issue policy (executive-owned)
4. Pay bonus annually; executive funds premium

## SEQUENCING DEPENDENCIES

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Confusing with SERP (different vehicle, different consequences)
- Not grossing up for taxes when the intent is to fully fund the benefit
- Underestimating exec's tax bracket effect

## COORDINATION NOTES
- **PSA Wealth:** product selection
- **CPA:** payroll/W-2 reporting
- **Attorney:** typically simple letter agreement

## CLIENT CONVERSATION FRAMING
> "Simplest structure: pay {executive} an extra bonus, deduct it as comp, they pay tax on it, they use what remains to fund a personal life insurance policy. They own everything immediately. Loss of retention pull is the trade-off — no vesting, no claw-back. For some executives that simplicity is what they value."

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- No vesting/clawback
- Tax inefficiency vs. SERP if retention goal exists
- Requires executive cooperation in establishing personal policy

## REFERENCES
- IRC §162 — business expense deduction
- Standard W-2 reporting
