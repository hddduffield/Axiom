### Natural-language explanation
Restricted §162 (sometimes "REBA") combines §162 deduction with retention through restricted access. Business pays bonus; executive funds policy; restrictive endorsement prevents executive from accessing cash value or surrendering until vesting. On separation pre-vesting, restriction enforces return or continuation.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Carrier doesn't support restrictive endorsements

## WHAT IT IS
§162 bonus arrangement with restrictive endorsement on the policy. Restricting party (typically employer) can prevent surrender, loan, or assignment until vesting conditions met. On full vesting, restriction lifts; executive has full ownership.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Marries §162 simplicity with retention pull. Executive's interest is contingent on continued service.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK
- Same as plain §162 plus retention value of vesting
- §162 deductible
- Vesting schedule typical: 5-10 year cliff or graded

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Define vesting schedule
2. Issue policy with restrictive endorsement
3. Annual bonus paid; premium funded; restriction in place
4. Monitor vesting milestones
5. Restriction lifts at full vesting
