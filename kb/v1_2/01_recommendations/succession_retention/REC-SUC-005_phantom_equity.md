# [REC-SUC-005] — Phantom Equity Plan

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Owner wants to give key employees economic upside without diluting ownership or governance
  - Possible transaction event ahead
  - §409A-compliant design feasible

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Owner intends to give actual equity (use REC-SUC-008)
  - 409A complexity unacceptable
```

### Natural-language explanation
"Phantom equity" units mirror the value of actual equity but don't carry voting rights or actual ownership. Holder receives cash payment(s) tied to value at vesting or transaction event. Useful for retention without dilution.

### Hard disqualifiers
- §409A non-compliance unavoidable

## WHAT IT IS
Contractual award of "units" valued like equity. Award document defines:
- Number of units
- Vesting schedule
- Triggering events for payment (transaction, retirement, death, departure)
- Valuation methodology
- §409A-compliant payment timing

Holder pays nothing for units; holder receives cash equivalent of equity value at trigger event. Award value is W-2 / ordinary income at payment.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Aligns key employees with owner's transaction outcome without giving them actual equity (and without diluting owner's interest). Simpler than actual equity for many businesses.

## VARIATIONS
- **Appreciation-only:** holder receives only growth above grant-date value (like SAR — REC-SUC-006)
- **Full-value:** holder receives total value at trigger
- **Performance-vested:** vesting tied to milestones beyond pure time

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Number of units × value at trigger
- Vesting schedule
- Tax: ordinary income at payout to holder; deductible to business

### Worked example
Business value $25M today; expected $50M at transaction in 5 years. Award COO 5% of phantom equity:
- Today's value: 5% × $25M = $1.25M
- Transaction value: 5% × $50M = $2.5M (full-value) or $1.25M appreciation (appreciation-only)
- COO receives cash at transaction; ordinary income; business deducts

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Define unit allocation and economics (full-value or appreciation-only)
2. Establish §409A-compliant plan document
3. Define triggering events and payment timing
4. Define valuation methodology (formula, appraisal, transaction-driven)
5. Award documents to executives
6. Annual valuation update for plan participant statements

## COMMON MISTAKES

- Valuation methodology not pre-defined; disputes at trigger
- Failing to coordinate with sale documents (buyer needs visibility)

## CLIENT CONVERSATION FRAMING
> "Phantom equity gives {executive(s)} a cash payout tied to {business_name}'s value at {trigger}. Doesn't dilute your ownership, doesn't give voting rights — just upside aligned with yours. ## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- §409A non-compliance has 20% penalty plus interest plus inclusion of prior deferrals
- Valuation disputes are common at trigger; pre-define methodology
- Buyer in transaction may want plan terminated or assumed; coordinate
