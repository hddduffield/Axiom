### Natural-language explanation
A written succession plan documents what happens to the business on owner's death, disability, or unplanned departure. Distinct from buy/sell (financial mechanics): succession addresses operational continuity — interim leadership, decision authority, customer/vendor communication, banking signatures.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Already has comprehensive plan

## WHAT IT IS
A document covering, at minimum:
- Interim leadership chain on owner death/disability
- Decision authority (who can sign contracts, approve spending, hire/fire)
- Banking signatories and access
- Customer/vendor communication protocol
- Coordination with buy/sell mechanics
- Family communication
- Annual review cadence

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
On owner death or disability without a written plan, the team is paralyzed for weeks while authority is sorted out — at exactly the moment value is most fragile. Written plan eliminates that paralysis.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Inventory operational decision points
2. Designate interim leadership and decision authority
3. Update banking signatures with backup designations
4. Customer / vendor communication scripts
5. Document; share with key team and family
6. Annual review

## PLAN OUTPUT TEMPLATE

> **Document the transition path.** A written succession plan covering both planned (sale, semi-retirement) and unplanned (death, disability) scenarios. The document defines who runs the business, who has signing authority, who has board control, and how decisions get made under each scenario. Distribute to key team members and family. Annual review and update. Distinct from the buy/sell — covers operational continuity through the financial transition period.
