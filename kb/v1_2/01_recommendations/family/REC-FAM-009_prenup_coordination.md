# [REC-FAM-009] — Prenuptial / Postnuptial Coordination

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ANY of:
  - Adult child planning marriage; substantial trust beneficiary or business stake involved
  - Existing marriage with substantial separate property at risk
  - Family wealth concentrated in marital communities
```

### Natural-language explanation
Adult children inheriting (or holding) material family assets are exposed to divorce risk affecting those assets. A prenuptial agreement (before marriage) or postnuptial (after) can preserve separate property treatment, define expectations, and reduce conflict potential.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Couple unwilling to engage
- Marriage immediate (insufficient time for proper drafting)

## WHAT IT IS
Premarital or postmarital agreement between spouses defining:
- Separate property identification
- Marital property formation rules
- Spousal support framework on dissolution
- Inheritance and gifting expectations
- Divorce procedure and asset division
- Disclosure requirements

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
For wealth flowing into the next generation, divorce risk can transfer family wealth out of the family. Prenup is the standard preservation tool, particularly when the inheriting spouse has substantial separate property going in.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Family conversation about expectation
2. Refer adult child to qualified family law attorney (NOT family's regular attorney; conflict)
3. Independent counsel for fiancé as well
4. Substantial pre-wedding lead time (months, not days)
5. Full disclosure between parties
6. Execution well before wedding

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Same attorney for both parties (unenforceable)
- Last-minute execution (duress arguments)
- Inadequate disclosure
- Ignoring postnup option for already-married couples

## COORDINATION NOTES
- **PSA Wealth:** advisor only; refer for execution
- **Family law attorney:** primary
- **Estate attorney:** coordinate with trust documents

## CLIENT CONVERSATION FRAMING
> "When {adult_child} marries, the trust assets and equity in {business_name} they'll eventually inherit are exposed to marital property issues. A prenup is the standard tool. Drafted by separate counsel for each side, executed months before the wedding. PSA doesn't draft these; we refer to family law specialists in their state."

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- Sensitive family conversation
- State law varies materially
- Drafting issues can render unenforceable
