## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - 529 account open 15+ years (or close to that milestone)
  - Beneficiary has earned income (rollover counts toward Roth annual limit)
  - 529 funded beyond likely education needs

DISQUALIFY if:
  - 529 not yet 15 years old
  - No beneficiary earned income
```

### Natural-language explanation
SECURE 2.0 added the ability to roll up to $35,000 (lifetime) from a 529 to the beneficiary's Roth IRA — subject to the 15-year account age requirement and the beneficiary's annual Roth contribution limit ($7,500 in 2026). Effectively converts excess 529 balances to retirement savings without the 10% non-qualified withdrawal penalty.

### Hard disqualifiers
- 529 too young (15-year requirement strict)
- Beneficiary lacks earned income

## WHAT IT IS
Tax-free, penalty-free direct rollover from 529 plan to the beneficiary's Roth IRA:
- Maximum lifetime: $35,000 per beneficiary
- Subject to annual Roth IRA contribution limits ($7,500 in 2026; $1,100 catch-up at 50+)
- 529 must be 15+ years old
- Contributions and earnings rolled within last 5 years are not eligible
- Beneficiary must have earned income equal to the rollover amount

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Provides flexibility for over-funded 529s. Excess can become next-gen retirement savings rather than incurring 10% penalty + ordinary income tax on non-qualified withdrawal.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Lifetime $35K rollover capacity per beneficiary
- Annual rollover up to Roth limit
- Tax-free Roth growth for next 30-50 years

### Worked example
Three over-funded 529s, each beneficiary at age 22 with earned income:
- Each beneficiary: $7,500/year × ~5 years = $35K rolled over
- Total: $105K of next-gen Roth capacity
- 40-year compound at 7%: ~$1.5M (combined) of tax-free retirement savings for the next generation

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Confirm 529 age (15+ years required)
2. Confirm beneficiary's earned income each year
3. Beneficiary opens Roth IRA if not already
4. Direct rollover (not indirect)
5. Track lifetime $35K limit per beneficiary
6. Multi-year cadence
