# [REC-FAM-010] — UTMA/UGMA Wind-Down Planning (Pre-Age-of-Majority)

## METADATA
- **ID:** REC-FAM-010
- **Status:** Active
- **Category:** Family
- **Engagement archetypes:** When existing UTMA in place
- **Plan section placement:** "Recommendations — Family"
- **Last verified:** April 2026

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Existing UTMA / UGMA accounts for children
  - Children approaching age of majority (18-21 by state)
  - Material balance ($25K+)
  - Owner concerned about unrestricted access at majority
```

### Natural-language explanation
UTMA/UGMA accounts transfer to the beneficiary at age of majority (18 or 21 by state). Substantial balances passing to a young adult unrestricted may not align with parents' intent. Pre-majority planning can shift assets to more controlled vehicles or accept unrestricted access with values-based preparation.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Already past age of majority
- Balance immaterial

## WHAT IT IS
Strategy options:
1. **Spend-down** for legitimate child benefits (education, transportation, computer, etc.) before majority
2. **529 conversion** — UTMA-to-529 transfer (note: 529 then has UTMA restrictions; partial benefit)
3. **Trust conversion** — convert UTMA to a custodial-trust structure with continued discretionary administration past majority (state-specific)
4. **Accept majority transfer** with preparation: financial education, gradual exposure
5. **Negotiated continuance** — agreement with adult child to maintain advisor management

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Substantial UTMA balances handed to an 18-21-year-old unrestricted is often not what parents intended. Pre-majority planning offers options.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Identify UTMA/UGMA balance and child's age
2. Determine state of UTMA's age of majority (18 or 21)
3. Choose strategy (spend-down, 529 transfer, trust conversion, etc.)
4. Execute pre-majority
5. Communicate with child as appropriate

## COMMON MISTAKES
- Realizing balance is large only when child is 17 — limited options
- UTMA-to-529 mechanics misunderstood (maintains UTMA restrictions)
- Failing to prepare child psychologically for transition

Best executed years before the transfer date."

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS
- State law varies on age of majority and conversion options
- UTMA can't be unwound (gift was completed)
