# [REC-FAM-003] — Custodial Roth IRA via Family Employment

## METADATA
- **ID:** REC-FAM-003
- **Status:** Active
- **Category:** Family

- **Plan section placement:** "Recommendations — Family"

## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Family-owned business
  - Children performing legitimate work for the business
  - Children paid W-2 wages (or 1099 in some cases)
  - Children have earned income to support Roth contribution

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Work is not genuinely performed (audit risk; child labor laws)
  - Child too young to perform legitimate tasks
```

### Natural-language explanation
Children employed in legitimate work in family business earn wages that support a Custodial Roth IRA. The wages are deductible to the business; child's W-2 income is largely tax-free up to the standard deduction; Roth contribution provides tax-free growth for 60+ years. Linked to REC-TAX-004 (family-employed children).

### Hard disqualifiers
- Work not genuinely performed
- Audit-risky pattern (paying for "work" not done)

## WHAT IT IS
Custodial Roth IRA in the child's name (custodian = parent or grandparent). Annual contribution up to lesser of $7,500 (2026) or child's earned income. Tax-free growth and distribution; flexibility for first-home purchase or qualified education before retirement age.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
60+ years of tax-free compounding. $7,500 contributed at age 14, compounded at 7% for 51 years (to age 65) = $230,000 from a single year's contribution. Multi-year contributions through high school and college years build substantial tax-free retirement balance.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Annual Roth contribution × tax-free compounding
- Long horizon (50+ years for young child)
- No tax leakage during accumulation or qualified distribution

### Worked example
Child employed at family business at age 14, $7,500 wages annually (within standard deduction):
- 8 years contributions through college (ages 14-21): $60,000 total
- Compounded to age 65 (~50 years more): ~$1.5M tax-free balance
- Tax-free for life

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Establish child's employment role with documented duties
2. Pay W-2 wages at reasonable rate for tasks (not above market)
3. Open Custodial Roth IRA at brokerage (Schwab, Fidelity)
4. Annual contribution up to earned income
5. File child's tax return (often $0 tax owed under standard deduction)

- Missing W-2 / payroll formality
