### Natural-language explanation
A family mission statement codifies the values, purposes, and operating principles that govern family wealth across generations. Used by trustees, advisors, and next-gen as a touchstone. Distinct from legal estate planning — addresses the "why" rather than the "what."

### Hard disqualifiers
- Wealth too small or transient to justify
- Family lacks coherent values to articulate

## WHAT IT IS
A written document, typically 1-3 pages:
- Origin story / how wealth was built
- Core family values
- Purpose for wealth (philanthropy, family stewardship, opportunity creation)
- Decision-making principles
- Communication norms
- Cadence for review and updates

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Without articulated values, next-gen inherits wealth without context. Mission statement gives trustees discretion language, helps next-gen make decisions consistent with family intent, and reduces conflict.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Family meeting (initial values discussion)
2. Draft document (often facilitated by external consultant)
3. Review and refinement across generations
4. Adopt; reference in trust documents and advisor relationships
5. Annual review
