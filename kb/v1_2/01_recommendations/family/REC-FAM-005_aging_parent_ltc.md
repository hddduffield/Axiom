### Natural-language explanation
For HNW clients with aging parents, late-life care can become a six- to seven-figure expense over multiple years — falling on the children if not insured. Pre-emptive planning: model expected need; coordinate parents' financial picture; evaluate LTC insurance for parents (while still insurable); plan ahead for cash-flow timing.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Parents fully resourced
- Parents in care already (planning past)

## WHAT IT IS
Family-level planning for parental care:
- Cost projection (assisted living + skilled nursing scenarios)
- Parents' financial capacity (their assets + Social Security)
- Gap analysis (what client supplies)
- LTC insurance evaluation if parents still insurable
- Documentation: powers of attorney, healthcare directives, family communication
- Coordination of POA and trustee for parents' assets

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Late-life parent care is a major hidden liability for HNW children. Pre-planning avoids forced asset sales, family conflict, and emergency-mode decisions.

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Components
- Projected care costs (range $200K-$1M+ over multi-year care periods)
- Parental capacity to absorb
- Family contribution gap
- LTC insurance benefit if obtained

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Conversation with parents about wishes and current situation
2. Financial picture analysis (their assets, income, insurance)
3. Cost projection scenarios (home care, assisted living, skilled nursing)
4. LTC insurance evaluation if eligible
5. Legal documentation (POA, healthcare directives, HIPAA, trust as needed)
6. Annual review

We model what care could cost ($300K-$1M+ over multiple years), what their resources cover, and what gap might fall on you. Where insurable, LTC for them now is much cheaper than the alternative. Documents — POA, healthcare directives — need to be current."

- Insurability at older ages limited
