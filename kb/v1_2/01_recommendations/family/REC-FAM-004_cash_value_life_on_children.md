### Natural-language explanation
Whole life or guaranteed UL on children, with parent (or trust) as policyholder. Builds cash value tax-deferred; insurability locked in young; can be transferred to child later. Premium typically modest ($2K-$10K/year). Trade-off: opportunity cost of those dollars in other vehicles.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Carrier won't issue
- Other vehicles preferred

## WHAT IT IS
Permanent life insurance on a child:
- Premium paid by parent or trust
- Cash value accumulates tax-deferred
- Transferable to child at age of majority or later
- Death benefit grows over time
- Insurability locked in regardless of future health changes

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT (cautiously)
Useful when:
- Family history suggests insurability concerns later
- Parent wants tax-favored vehicle outside conventional retirement
- Long-term wealth transfer to child planned

## QUANTIFIED IMPACT FRAMEWORK

### Worked example
Whole life on age-5 child, $5K/year premium for 20 years:
- $100K premium paid
- Cash value at age 25: ~$140K-$170K (varies by carrier)
- Death benefit: ~$300K-$400K
- Insurability: locked in
- Transferable to child at age 21+ (gift; uses annual exclusion or lifetime exemption)

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Underwrite child (insurability)
2. Choose carrier and policy structure
3. Owner: parent, grandparent, or ILIT (if estate planning relevant)
4. Annual premium funded
5. Annual review

- Comparing to investment returns rather than to other tax-favored vehicles
- Neglecting to review at child's age of majority

## CAVEATS & DISQUALIFIERS

- Carrier quality matters for long-term policy
- Surrender penalties early years
