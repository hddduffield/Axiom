## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Multi-gen family wealth
  - Adult children with stake in family wealth or operations
  - No existing meeting structure

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Family already meets formally
  - Family dynamics make formal cadence counterproductive
```

### Natural-language explanation
Periodic family meetings (annual or semi-annual) bring family members together to discuss wealth, values, philanthropy, and shared decisions. Especially important for multi-gen families with shared trust beneficiaries or family business stakes.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Severe family conflict
- Wealth too small to justify

## WHAT IT IS
Structured family meetings on a defined cadence:
- Annual or semi-annual
- Agenda: financial state, philanthropy, business performance (if family business), family member updates
- Participation rules (age threshold, spouse inclusion, etc.)
- External facilitator often used initially
- Documentation of decisions

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Builds shared understanding across generations. Reduces conflict via early communication. Prepares next-gen for decisions they'll inherit.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Define participants and cadence
2. First meeting agenda
3. Facilitator (external for first 1-3 meetings often valuable)
4. Norms (confidentiality, decision-making)
5. Annual review of meeting structure
