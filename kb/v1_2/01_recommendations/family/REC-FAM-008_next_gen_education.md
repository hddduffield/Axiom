## TRIGGERING CONDITIONS

### Structured logic
```
TRIGGER if ALL of:
  - Children/grandchildren approaching distribution ages (typically 21-35)
  - Material trust distributions or business stake
  - No existing structured financial education

DISQUALIFY if:
  - Children too young
  - Children have demonstrated financial competence
```

### Natural-language explanation
Children inheriting material wealth without financial literacy face high risk: poor investment decisions, loss of family wealth, relationship damage with family. A structured education program — gradual exposure to budgeting, investment, tax, estate concepts — prepares them.

### Hard disqualifiers
- Existing financial sophistication

## WHAT IT IS
Multi-year program, scaled by age:
- Teen years: budgeting, saving, basic investing
- Late teens/college: spending plans, investment basics, tax filing introduction
- Early 20s: investment strategy, tax planning, debt management
- Mid 20s+: estate concepts, family wealth context, philanthropy

PSA-led or external advisor-led. Often integrated with annual family meetings.

## WHY WE RECOMMEND IT
Prepares next-gen for decisions they'll inherit. Reduces "wealth shock" at distribution. Aligns next-gen with family stewardship intent.

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS
1. Curriculum scaled to ages
2. Annual touchpoints (formal or informal)
3. Increasing engagement with family financial information at appropriate ages
4. Periodic 1-on-1 advisor relationships beginning at appropriate age
